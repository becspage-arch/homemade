# Cooking technique backlog

> This is the foundational technique reference library. Recipes (complete
> dishes) live in `docs/recipe-backlog.md`. Anything you'd describe as
> "a recipe for X" belongs there, not here.

The technique map for cooking. Each entry is a standalone reference
tutorial a reader consults to learn HOW — knife skills, kit deep-dives,
ingredient deep-dives, mother sauces, foundation breads and pastries,
food-safety primers, and the method chapters that recipes rely on.

Recipes themselves (specific dishes — lasagna, sourdough country loaf,
strawberry jam, beef bourguignon, chicken tikka masala) are not here.
They live in `docs/recipe-backlog.md`, which drives bulk authoring.

Difficulty tags match the `Difficulty` enum in
`packages/db/prisma/schema.prisma` (BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED).
Season tags match the `Season` enum (SPRING / SUMMER / AUTUMN / WINTER /
YEAR_ROUND); a missing season means year-round.

Public-domain source pointers are cited by Project Gutenberg ID where
available. PG IDs were correct at time of writing; verify before quoting
in a tutorial body. USDA / extension service material is in the public
domain in the US (works of federal employees) and used as the spine for
food-safety guidance.

## Source coverage gaps

A handful of areas where public-domain coverage is thin:

- **Modern fermentation science.** Sandor Katz and Noma-era technique are
  under copyright. The Victorian record covers traditional vinegars, ginger
  beer, and country wines well, but not modern lacto-fermented vegetable
  technique. Synthesise from USDA / extension service safety material plus
  cross-referenced contemporary cookery practice.
- **Sous vide and modernist technique.** No public-domain literature.
  Synthesise from food-science journals and university extension where they
  publish under PD.
- **Allergen handling and special diets.** Modern allergy science is under
  copyright. Lean on NHS / NIH PD guidance.
- **Equilibrium curing for charcuterie.** The Victorian dry-cure method is
  well-documented, but the modern percentage-based equilibrium approach is
  under copyright. Cite USDA on cure-salt safety, synthesise the method.
- **Regional UK and Irish cookery.** Many regional dishes are recorded only
  in 20th-century books still under copyright. Pre-1928 sources lean
  metropolitan-English; Welsh, Cornish, Irish, Scottish regional cookery is
  thinner in the PD record.
- **Non-European cuisines.** PD cookery is heavily European. South Asian,
  East Asian, West African, Caribbean recipes need careful sourcing — much
  of what we want to cover came into English-language print after 1928. Use
  recipes only where they are clearly traditional and cross-reference
  multiple modern sources.
- **Korean, Vietnamese, Thai, and modern regional Indian cookery.** The
  pre-1928 English-language PD record is effectively empty for these
  cuisines. The v1 recipe backlog defers most modern regional non-European
  cookery (see `docs/recipe-backlog.md` → "Deferred to v2"). Where v1
  recipes brush against these cuisines (Anglo-Indian curry-house dishes,
  Tex-Mex Anglicised Mexican cookery), source attribution shifts to
  SYNTHESISED or CLASSIC rather than PUBLIC_DOMAIN.

The bot-as-editor pass and the spot-check rhythm absorb these gaps. Where a
PD source is thin, the source attribution shifts to SYNTHESISED or CLASSIC
rather than PUBLIC_DOMAIN.

## Public-domain source pointers

The spine for technique-tutorial sourcing:

- Mrs Beeton's *Book of Household Management* (1861, PG #10136) — broad
  coverage of British technique, foundations, sauces, preserves, pastries.
- Eliza Acton, *Modern Cookery for Private Families* (1845, PD UK) —
  English bread and the introductory technique chapters.
- Hannah Glasse, *The Art of Cookery Made Plain and Easy* (1747, PG
  #51289) — older British technique, condiments, the original ketchups.
- Escoffier, *Le Guide Culinaire* (1903, PD France) — exhaustive on
  sauces, mother-sauce family, stocks, technique.
- Pellegrino Artusi, *La scienza in cucina e l'arte di mangiar bene*
  (1891, PD Italy) — Italian technique and traditional method.
- Brillat-Savarin, *Physiologie du goût* (1825, PD France) — the
  fundamentals of taste and the physiology references.
- Fannie Farmer, *The Boston Cooking-School Cook Book* (1896, PD US) —
  American technique baseline, weighing-versus-volume tradition.
- The Settlement Cook Book (1901, PD US).
- The White House Cook Book (1887, PD US).
- USDA *Food Safety and Inspection Service* publications (PD US, perpetual)
  — the food-safety spine. Cure salts, processing times, smoking rules.
- USDA *Complete Guide to Home Canning* (latest edition, PD US) — the
  authoritative spine for preservation safety.
- National Center for Home Food Preservation (NCHFP) at University of
  Georgia (PD US extension service) — current safe-acidity and processing
  times for jams, jellies, pickles, ferments.
- Penn State Extension on home cheesemaking (PD).
