# Cooking content backlog

The territory map for cooking. Each sub-category lists the tutorials we intend
to publish, with public-domain source pointers and difficulty / season tags
where they apply. The orchestrator plans batches against this file. It is not
the authoring brief — that lives in `docs/tutorial-author.md` once we get to
the pilot batch.

Difficulty tags match the `Difficulty` enum in `packages/db/prisma/schema.prisma`
(BEGINNER / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED). Season tags match the `Season` enum
(SPRING / SUMMER / AUTUMN / WINTER / YEAR_ROUND); a missing season means
year-round.

Public-domain source pointers are cited by Project Gutenberg ID where available.
PG IDs were correct at time of writing; verify before quoting in a tutorial body.
USDA / extension service material is in the public domain in the US (works of
federal employees) and used as the spine for food-safety guidance.

## Source coverage gaps

A handful of areas where public-domain coverage is thin:

- **Modern fermentation science.** Sandor Katz and Noma-era technique are
  under copyright. The Victorian record covers traditional vinegars, ginger
  beer, and country wines well, but not modern lacto-fermented vegetable
  technique. Synthesise from USDA / extension service safety material plus
  cross-referenced contemporary cookery practice.
- **Sous vide and modernist technique.** No public-domain literature.
  Synthesise from food-science journals and university extension where they
  publish under PD.
- **Allergen handling and special diets.** Modern allergy science is under
  copyright. Lean on NHS / NIH PD guidance.
- **Equilibrium curing for charcuterie.** The Victorian dry-cure method is
  well-documented, but the modern percentage-based equilibrium approach is
  under copyright. Cite USDA on cure-salt safety, synthesise the method.
- **Regional UK and Irish cookery.** Many regional dishes are recorded only
  in 20th-century books still under copyright. Pre-1928 sources lean
  metropolitan-English; Welsh, Cornish, Irish, Scottish regional cookery is
  thinner in the PD record.
- **Non-European cuisines.** PD cookery is heavily European. South Asian,
  East Asian, West African, Caribbean recipes need careful sourcing — much
  of what we want to cover came into English-language print after 1928. Use
  recipes only where they are clearly traditional and cross-reference
  multiple modern sources.
- **Korean cookery.** The expansion pulled in kimchi variants, ssamjang,
  doenjang, gochujang sauce base, samgyeopsal, bossam, sundubu jjigae, kalguksu,
  galbi-jjim, gaji-namul, kongnamul muchim, hotteok pancakes, gyeran-jjim, and
  more. There is effectively no pre-1928 English-language PD record for Korean
  cookery; rely on cross-referenced modern sources and synthesise.
- **Vietnamese cookery.** Phở, bún bò Huế, bò kho, cà phê sữa đá, đồ chua,
  chả cá lã vọng, nước chấm, thịt kho, gừng và nước mắm — all post-1928 in
  the English-language record. Synthesise from cross-referenced modern
  sources.
- **Caribbean cookery.** Rundown, jerk, bounce, mauby, ackee — same problem.
  Use named-tradition sources (Jamaica Information Service PD material where
  available) and cross-reference.
- **South-East Asian.** Pad thai, satay, soto ayam, tinola, bun bo Hue,
  rendang, massaman, khao soi — virtually no PD record. Synthesise.
- **Indian regional cookery beyond Mrs Beeton's "Indian" section.** Mrs
  Beeton has curry-powder recipes and a short Indian section; Hannah Glasse
  describes the original "curry" in English. Beyond that, Hyderabadi
  biriyani, Goan vindaloo and xacuti, Bengali fish curries, Kerala meen
  moilee, Punjabi karahi, and the wider regional spectrum mostly post-date
  PD coverage. Use Edward Palmer's *Indian Cookery* (1880s, PD) and
  cross-reference modern regional cookbooks.
- **Latin American cookery.** Carnitas, cochinita pibil, lechon asado,
  pupusas, arepas, empanadas Argentinas, atole de chocolate — limited PD.
  Synthesise.
- **Japanese cookery beyond dashi and basic technique.** Castella has a
  trail through Portuguese-Japanese contact pre-1928. Modern Japanese home
  cooking (tonkatsu, katsu curry, tamagoyaki, onsen tamago, gyoza, ramen)
  is post-1928 in English. Synthesise from cross-referenced sources.

The bot-as-editor pass and the spot-check rhythm absorb these gaps. Where a
PD source is thin, the source attribution shifts to SYNTHESISED or CLASSIC
rather than PUBLIC_DOMAIN.
