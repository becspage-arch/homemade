## Method

Preheat the oven to 350 F (180 C / 160 Fan / Gas 4), and line a baking sheet with baking paper.

Cream together the butter, white sugar and brown sugar in a medium sized mixing bowl. This usually takes 2-3 mins depending on how strong your mixer is. Stop when you can see the mixture is fully mixed together, with no visible sugar granules. It will also be slightly fluffy and visibly lighter in color than when you started.

Add the egg & vanilla and mix until combined.

In a separate bowl, sift and combine the flour, cocoa, hot chocolate powder, baking soda and salt.  Gently mix so it is combined. Strictly speaking, this step is optional. However having this combined before you add it to the rest of the mixture generally results in cookies with a more even texture.

Add the flour mixture to the rest of the mixture and stir until it is combined. Pour roughly ¼ of the flour in at a time, mixing until only just combined, then add more. Doing this gives a smoother batter. Do not over mix or the cookies end up flat. Stir less than you think you need to.

Add the milk and stir to combine. The dough should now be ready to scoop into portions.

Scoop into portions and shape as desired.

For the size shown here, I use approx 30g of dough per cookie. However you can also eyeball it. If you use smaller portions, you will need to use a shorter cooking time. Larger portions will take slightly longer to bake. I roll into balls, then shape to be more cylindrical. I then cook them with the cylinder pointing upwards to result in a taller cookie as it melts down onto itself.

OPTIONAL MATURING - Put the portions into a container and refrigerate for 1-3 days. Doing this matures the dough and results in a deeper taste and taller cookie. You can leave longer but I’ve found 3 days gives the optimal shape and best taste. If you are freezing, make sure to refrigerate before you freeze. If you are not going to do this step, make sure to quickly refrigerate for at least 30 mins. That helps the cookie dough keep it’s shape during the cooking.

OPTIONAL FREEZING - If you wish to freeze, do this now. I usually place them on baking paper on a baking tray, wait until they are frozen and transfer to a labelled plastic bag. You do not need to defrost before baking, just place straight into the oven and add 2-3 mins to the cooking time. Then continue as normal.

Bake for 10 minutes only. The cookies will look a little uncooked still. This is correct.

Take the cookies out of the oven and gently press ½ a large marshmallow onto the top of each cookie. Put them bake in the oven and bake for another 2 minutes. The edges of the cookies should start to form small cracks or look set at this point. When this happens, remove from the oven.

OPTIONAL SHAPING - While still hot, use a round cookie cutter and place over each cookie. Swirl the cookie cutter gently to shape into a perfect circle. Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet to set and finish cooking in the middle (or eat while warm and enjoy it nice and gooey!).

Let the cookies fully cool on the baking tray.

OPTIONAL ICING - Melt the chocolate. You can either use a double boiler, or a microwave. If you are using a double boiler, follow the manufacturer’s instructions. If you are using a microwave, put into a microwaveable bowl, and cover with clingfilm. Microwave on high for 20-30 seconds at a time, stirring in between. When fully melted, spoon enough chocolate over each cookie to just cover the marshmallow (or leave some exposed if you wish).

OPTIONAL GARNISH - Press mini marshmallows or sprinkles into the icing and / or drizzle melted white chocolate over the cookies as desired.
