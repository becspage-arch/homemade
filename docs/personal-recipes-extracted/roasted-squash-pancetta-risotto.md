# Roasted Squash & Pancetta Risotto

> source: master.docx · section: DINNERS

## Ingredients

- Basic risotto
- 1 litre vegetable or chicken stock
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 large onion, peeled and finely chopped
- 4-5 sticks of celery, trimmed and finely chopped
- 600g / 1lb 6oz risotto rice
- 250ml / 9fl oz vermouth or dry white wine

### Squash ingredients

- 1 butternut squash, carefully quartered and seeds discarded
- 1 cinnamon stick, ground
- 1 dried red chilli, ground (optional)
- 700ml hot vegetable or chicken stock
- 1 tablespoon mascarpone cheese (or vegan alternative)
- 100g butter (or vegan butter)
- 1-2 handfuls of grated parmesan (or vegan alternative)
- 12 slices of pancetta or dry-cured smoky bacon
- 1 bunch of fresh sage, leaves picked
- 2 handfuls of freshly grated Parmesan cheese
- extra virgin olive oil

## Method

Preheat your oven to 200°C/400°F/gas 6.

In an ovenproof dish, toss the squash wedges with a little olive oil, salt and pepper, cinnamon and optionally some chilli.

Roast for 30 minutes, or until soft.

Bring the stock to simmer in a saucepan

Put the olive oil and butter in a separate large pan

Add the onion and celery and cook very gently for about 15 minutes, without colouring, until soft.

Add the rice (it will sizzle) and turn up the heat. Keep stirring so it doesn’t catch.

Quickly pour in the vermouth or wine. Keep stirring until it has evaporated.

Add the stock to the rice a ladle at a time, waiting until it has been fully absorbed before adding the next ladle. Keep the heat low.

Put a large saucepan on medium to high heat, and pour in half the stock, followed by all of the risotto base.

Scoop the flesh out of the squash skin and break it in.

Stirring all the time, gently bring to the oil, then turn down the heat and simmer until almost all the stock has been absorbed.

Add the rest of the stock one ladleful at a time until the rice is cooked.

Turn off the heat, beat in the mascarpone, butter and parmesan.

Add salt & pepper

Fry the pancetta in a generous amount of olive oil until crispy, then rest on a paper towel.

Fry the sage leaves in the same pan for a minute or two until crispy.

Serve risotto with sage leaves and pancetta on top. Parmesan to grate on.
