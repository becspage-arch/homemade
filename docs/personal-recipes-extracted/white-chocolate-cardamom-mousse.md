# White Chocolate Cardamom Mousse (Not Dairy-Free)

> source: master.docx · section: DESSERTS

## Ingredients

- 8 green cardamom pods
- 100ml milk
- 3 bay leaves
- 300ml double cream
- 3 free range egg whites

## Method

Break cardamom pods open and extract seeds (throw husk). Crush the seeds lightly then add to the milk and bay leaves in a small saucepan. Heat until it approaches boiling point then remove.Meanwhile, break chocolate into small pieces and melt in a bain marie.Whip the cream so it forms soft peaks. Beat the egg whites to stiff peaks.When the chocolate is completely melted remove from heat and combine with the warm milk, which should be sieved first to remove the bay leaves and seeds. Once it is a velvety texture  scoop the mixture on to the stiffly beaten egg whites and fold gently with a metal spoon. Now add the whipped cream.Scrape mixture into a serving bowl or espresso cups and refrigerate for 3-4 hours until set (I think if you've used an electric whisk your mousse will probably set firmer than mine did! What's that about a bad workman blaming his tools..hmm) Dust with cocoa powder or do a dark chocolate sauce with it as I did -

175 g dark chocolate

50g butter

50g caster sugar

2 tbsp golden syrup

200ml milk

Melt the chocolate and butter in a bain marie. Stir in the sugar and syrup until dissolved (I put less in than is recommended above as the mousse is so sweet I think it needs to be a bit more bitter to balance it, then pour in the milk and continue to cook stirring often until the sauce thickens.
