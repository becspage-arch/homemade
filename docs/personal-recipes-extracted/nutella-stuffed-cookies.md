## Method

Preheat the oven to 350 F (180 C / 160 Fan / Gas 4), and line a baking sheet with baking paper.

Cream together the butter and sugar in a medium sized mixing bowl. This usually takes 2-5 mins depending on how strong your mixer is. Stop when you can see the mixture is fully mixed together, with no visible sugar granules. It will also be slightly fluffy and visibly lighter in color than when you started.

Add the egg & vanilla and mix until combined.

In a separate bowl, sift and combine the flour, baking soda and salt. Gently mix so it is combined.

TIP - Strictly speaking, this step is optional. However having this combined before you add it to the rest of the mixture generally results in cookies with a more even texture..

Add the flour mixture to the rest of the mixture and stir until it is combined.

TIP - Pour roughly ¼ of the flour in at a time, mixing until only just combined, then add more. Doing this gives a smoother batter. Do not over mix or the cookies end up flat. Stir less than you think you need to.

Pour in the chocolate chips. Stir gently to fold them into the batter. This should finish the mixing and it should now be ready to scoop into portions.

Scoop into portions and roll into balls. Then squish your fingers or a spoon into the middle of each ball. Shape with your fingertips to form a small bowl shape.

TIPS

For the size shown here, I use approx 50g of dough per cookie. However you can also eyeball it.

If you use smaller portions, you will need to use a shorter cooking time. Larger portions will take slightly longer to bake.

I roll into balls, then shape to be more cylindrical. I then cook them with the cylinder pointing upwards to result in a taller cookie as it melts down onto itself.

If you find the dough too sticky or soft to shape, put the rough balls into the fridge for 30mins to firm up, then continue with shaping.

Add a heaped teaspoon of nutella to the middle of each dough bowl.

Shape the cookie dough up over the nutella and pinch it closed. I find it’s easier to imagine it’s a dumpling and pinch it to the side, then shape from there.

Gently roll it into a circular shape. The aim is to make sure all the nutella stays hidden inside.

Place cookie portions in the fridge for minimum 30 minutes to firm up and mature.

OPTIONAL - Leave portions in the fridge for 1-3 days. Doing this matures the dough and results in a deeper taste and taller cookie. You can leave longer but I’ve found 3 days gives the optimal shape and best taste. If you skip this step, make sure to refrigerate for at least 30 minutes just to firm up the dough before baking. If you are freezing, make sure to refrigerate before you freeze.

OPTIONAL - If you wish to freeze, do this now. I usually place them on baking paper on a baking tray, wait until they are frozen and transfer to a labelled plastic bag. You do not need to defrost before baking, just place straight into the oven and add 2-3 mins to the cooking time.

Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges start to form small cracks or look set, then remove from the oven.

OPTIONAL - While still hot, use a round cookie cutter and place over each cookie. Swirl the cookie cutter gently to shape into a perfect circle.

Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet to set and finish cooking in the middle (or eat while warm and enjoy it nice and gooey!).
