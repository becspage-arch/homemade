# Giant Chocolate Cornflake Cookies

> source: master.docx · section: BAKING + TREATS · Prep time: 20 min

## Method

Preheat the oven to 350 F (180 C / 160 Fan / Gas 4), and line a baking sheet with baking paper.

Cream together the butter and sugar in a medium sized mixing bowl. This usually takes 2-5 mins depending on how strong your mixer is. Stop when you can see the mixture is fully mixed together, with no visible sugar granules. It will also be slightly fluffy and visibly lighter in color than when you started.

In a separate bowl, sift and combine the flour, cornflour and cocoa. Gently mix so it is combined.

TIP - Strictly speaking, this step is optional. However having this combined before you add it to the rest of the mixture generally results in cookies with a more even texture..

Add the flour mixture to the rest of the mixture and stir until it is combined.

TIP - Pour roughly ¼ of the flour in at a time, mixing until only just combined, then add more. Doing this gives a smoother batter. Do not over mix or the cookies end up flat. Stir less than you think you need to.

Pour in the cornflakes. Stir gently to fold them into the batter. This should finish the mixing and it should now be ready to scoop into portions.

Scoop into portions and shape as desired.

TIPS

For the size shown here, I use approx 80g of dough per cookie. However you can also eyeball it.

If you use smaller portions, you will need to use a shorter cooking time. Larger portions will take slightly longer to bake.

I roll into balls, then shape to be more cylindrical. I then cook them with the cylinder pointing upwards to result in a taller cookie as it melts down onto itself.

OPTIONAL - Put the portions into a container and refrigerate for 1-3 days. Doing this matures the dough and results in a deeper taste and taller cookie. You can leave longer but I’ve found 3 days gives the optimal shape and best taste. If you are freezing, make sure to refrigerate before you freeze.

OPTIONAL - If you wish to freeze, do this now. I usually place them on baking paper on a baking tray, wait until they are frozen and transfer to a labelled plastic bag. You do not need to defrost before baking, just place straight into the oven and add 2-3 mins to the cooking time.

Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the edges start to form small cracks or look set, then remove from the oven.

OPTIONAL - While still hot, use a round cookie cutter and place over each cookie. Swirl the cookie cutter gently to shape into a perfect circle. Add either an additional whole, or some crushed, cookie & cream biscuits to the top for decoration.

Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet to set and finish cooking in the middle (or eat while warm and enjoy it nice and gooey!).

OPTIONAL ICING

Beat the butter until creamy for about 2 minutes using a hand or stand mixer.

Add the vanilla and milk and mix to combine.

In a separate bowl, sift the cocoa powder, and mix in the icing powder so they are combined.

Add the cocoa mixture to the butter mixture and gently mix to combine.

Once combined, increase the speed of the mixture to high and beat for 2-3 minutes, until combined and fluffy.

TIP - If the icing is too thick, add a little extra milk. If it is too thin, add a little extra icing sugar.

TIP - The icing will keep in the fridge for up to a week or can be frozen for up to 3 months. Make sure to allow it to return to room temperature, then beat it for 30 seconds to 1min to prepare it for use.

Either spread & smooth the icing directly onto the cookies, or put into a piping bag and pipe on.

OPTIONAL GARNISH - Sprinkle cornflakes over the cookies and press very gently to set them into the icing.
