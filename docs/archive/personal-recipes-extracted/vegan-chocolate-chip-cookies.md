## Method

Preheat the oven to 350 F (180 C / 160 Fan / Gas 4), and line a baking sheet with baking paper.

Cream together the butter, brown sugar and caster sugar in a medium sized mixing bowl. This usually takes 2-5 mins depending on how strong your mixer is. Stop when you can see the mixture is fully mixed together, with no visible sugar granules. It will also be slightly fluffy and visibly lighter in color than when you started.

Mix in the vanilla and milk.

In a separate bowl, sift and combine the flour, cornflour and baking powder. Gently mix so it is combined.

TIP - Strictly speaking, this step is optional. However having this combined before you add it to the rest of the mixture generally results in cookies with a more even texture..

Add the flour mixture to the rest of the mixture and stir until it is combined.

TIP - Do not over mix or the cookies end up flat. Stir less than you think you need to.

Scoop into portions and shape as desired.

TIPS

For the size shown here, I use approx 25-30g of dough per cookie. However you can also eyeball it.

If you use smaller portions, you will need to use a shorter cooking time. Larger portions will take slightly longer to bake.

OPTIONAL - Put the portions into a container and refrigerate for 1-3 days. Doing this matures the dough and results in a deeper taste and taller cookie. You can leave longer but I’ve found 3 days gives the optimal shape and best taste. At minimum, I usually refrigerate for 30 mins to just firm the dough up. If you are freezing, make sure to refrigerate before you freeze.

OPTIONAL - If you wish to freeze, do this now. I usually place them on baking paper on a baking tray, wait until they are frozen and transfer to a labelled plastic bag. You do not need to defrost before baking, just place straight into the oven and add 2-3 mins to the cooking time.

Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges start to go slightly golden, then remove from the oven. As the cookies cool, they will darken in colour so they are usually ready as soon as the edges begin to go golden.

OPTIONAL - While still hot, use a round cookie cutter and place over each cookie. Swirl the cookie cutter gently to shape into a perfect circle.

OPTIONAL - Add additional chocolate chips (or anything else you fancy) to the top of each cookie for decoration.

Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet to set and finish cooking in the middle (or eat while warm and enjoy it nice and gooey!).
