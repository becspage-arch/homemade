# Cookies & Cream Cookies

> source: master.docx · section: BAKING + TREATS · Prep time: 15 min

## Ingredients

- I’ve included ingredients for a small batch, or bulk batch. Use whichever you prefer.
- You can use any brand cookie & cream biscuits. I use regular Oreos.
- Small Batch (makes 12 cookies)
- 12x (approx 135g) cookie & cream biscuits
- ½ cup (115g) unsalted butter, softened
- ½ cup (100g) white sugar
- ½ cup (100g) brown sugar
- 1 egg
- 1 teaspoon vanilla
- 1 ½ cup (188g) flour
- 1 teaspoon baking soda
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 cup (170g) chocolate chips
- Bulk Batch (makes 48 cookies)
- 48x (approx 540g) cookie & cream biscuits
- 2 cups (460g) unsalted butter, softened
- 2 cups (400g) white sugar
- 2 cups (400g) brown sugar
- 4 eggs
- 4 teaspoons vanilla
- 6 cups (752g) flour
- 4 teaspoons baking soda
- 4 teaspoons salt
- 4 cups (680g) chocolate chips

## Method

Preheat the oven to 350 F (180 C / 160 Fan / Gas 4), and line a baking sheet with baking paper.

Roughly crush the cookie & cream biscuits. To do this, either do a quick pulse in your food processor. Or put them into a plastic bag and gently hit with a rolling pin to break into pieces. Place aside for later.

Cream together the butter, white sugar and brown sugar in a medium sized mixing bowl. This usually takes 2-5 mins depending on how strong your mixer is. Stop when you can see the mixture is fully mixed together, with no visible sugar granules. It will also be slightly fluffy and visibly lighter in color than when you started.

Add the egg and vanilla to the butter & sugar mixture, and mix well.

In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking soda and salt. Gently mix so the baking soda & salt is combined with the flour.

TIP - Strictly speaking, this step is optional. However having this combined before you add it to the rest of the mixture generally results in cookies that rise more evenly.

Add the flour mixture to the rest of the mixture and stir until it is combined.

TIP - Pour roughly ¼ of the flour in at a time, mixing until only just combined, then add more. Doing this gives a smoother batter. Do not over mix or the cookies end up flat. Stir less than you think you need to.

Add the crushed cookie & cream biscuits, and chocolate chips. Stir gently to fold them into the batter. This should finish the mixing and it should now be ready to scoop into portions.

Scoop into portions and shape as desired.

TIPS

I use a 2 tablespoon (1oz / 28g) scoop to do this. However you can also eyeball it.

If you use smaller portions, you will need to use a shorter cooking time. Larger portions will take slightly longer to bake.

I roll into balls, then shape to be more cylindrical. I then cook them with the cylinder pointing upwards to result in a taller cookie as it melts down onto itself.

OPTIONAL - Put the portions into a container and refrigerate for 1-3 days. Doing this matures the dough and results in a deeper taste and taller cookie. You can leave longer but I’ve found 3 days gives the optimal shape and best taste. If you are freezing, make sure to refrigerate before you freeze.

OPTIONAL - If you wish to freeze, do this now. I usually place them on baking paper on a baking tray, wait until they are frozen and transfer to a labelled plastic bag. You do not need to defrost before baking, just place straight into the oven and add 2-3 mins to the cooking time.

Bake for 10-12 minutes or until the edges turn golden brown, then remove from the oven.

OPTIONAL - While still hot, use a round cookie cutter and place over each cookie. Swirl the cookie cutter gently to shape into a perfect circle. Add either an additional whole, or some crushed, cookie & cream biscuits to the top for decoration.

Let the cookies cool completely on the baking sheet to set and finish cooking in the middle (or eat while warm and enjoy it nice and gooey!).
