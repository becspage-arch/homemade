# Beef Bourguignon

> source: master.docx · section: DINNERS

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp plain flour
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 900g (2lb) top rib or braising steak, cut into cubes
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 25g (1 oz) butter, or vegan butter
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed
- 115g (4oz) streaky bacon
- 2 leeks, thickly sliced
- 2 carrots, thickly sliced (I add more like 6-8 carrots to fill it out)
- 18 pickling onions or shallots, peeled
- 300ml beef stock
- 450ml red burgundy wine
- 2 tbsp fresh bouquet garni of thyme, parsley, bay leaf and oregano chopped
- 1 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped

## Method

Preheat the oven to 180C / 350F / Gas 4.

Place the flour, salt and pepper into a large polythene bag and shake to mix well.

Add the beef cubes and shake to coat evenly.

Heat the oil and butter in a large frying pan until very hot.

Add the beef and any remaining flour and fry quickly until evenly browned.

Remove the beef using a slotted spoon and place in a large casserole dish.

Add the garlic and bacon to the pan and fry for 2-3 min until lightly browned.

Stir in the carrots, leeks and onions and continue frying for 2-3 minutes until lightly browned.

Add the stock, 300ml of the red wine and the bouquet garni.

Bring to the boil, then pour over the beef. Stir to mix well.

Cover and cook in the oven for about 2 hours or until the beef is tender (I cook for 5-8 hours, adding water as needed to keep the meat and vegetables covered).

Stir in the remaining wine and parsley.

Serve hot with crusty bread, rice or noodles.
