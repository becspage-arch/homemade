# Mindset brainstorm — life categories × stuck-on × practice types

The matrix that drives the Mindset library. Each cell can produce 5–10
library entries. This file is the working brainstorm — extended as
Rebecca and Claude walk through it. The output feeds the Mindset
backlog (the equivalent of `docs/recipe-backlog.md`).

## How this works

Three axes:

1. **Life category** (Money, Body, Motherhood, Sleep, etc.) — 16 at full
   scope, listed below.
2. **Stuck-on points** — the specific places someone gets caught within
   the category. Brainstorm exhaustively; specificity is the moat
   ("the money block where you earn well but can't hold it" beats
   "money blocks").
3. **Practice type** — the format the work takes. 11 types in the
   current taxonomy.

Each (category × stuck-on) cell can have multiple practice-type entries.
A single stuck-on point — say "wanting huge wealth but can't picture
yourself in it" — produces a tapping script, an energy alignment
statement, an affirmation, a spell, a ritual, an activity, a journal
prompt, a visualisation, sometimes an embodiment piece, plus a reading
that explains the block. Nine practices from one stuck-on point. Times
30 stuck-on points per category × 16 categories ≈ **3,000–4,000
library entries at full depth**.

## Practice types (11)

1. Tapping (EFT) scripts
2. Energy alignment statements — Money Zone–style
3. Affirmations — single repeatable lines
4. Spells — explicitly magical (bay leaf, candle, sigil, bath)
5. Rituals — embodied energetic without the magical frame
6. Activities — embodied non-energetic
7. Journal prompts — one tight question per
8. Visualisations — short guided imagery
9. Meditations — sitting practices
10. Embodiment — mirror, body acknowledgement, dressing-for
11. Reading / education — long-form

## Cross-category tagging

Many entries naturally tag multiple categories. The schema's
`targets[]` array supports this. Examples:

- "Trauma + parenting (passing it on)" → Fear + Motherhood
- "Pregnancy / postpartum body" → Body + Motherhood
- "Spouse-money disagreements" → Money + Relationships
- "Grief and being a mother" → Grief + Motherhood
- "Loss of identity — kids leaving" → Grief + Motherhood + Ageing
- "Sex after a certain age" → Body + Ageing + Relationships

Primary category for each entry, plus secondary tags. Discovery
surfaces show entries in their primary category but they appear in
search and matcher across all their tagged categories.

## Naming conventions

**Categories** stay plain and direct (Money, Body, Sleep, Motherhood).

**Individual practices** are named for what they do — functional, not
clever. Tapping scripts: "Tapping for the money leak", "Tapping for
'others can, not me'". Rituals: "The Wealth Identity Embodiment".
Affirmations: "I am the woman with cash and assets pouring in".
Reading entries: "Why huge wealth feels impossible". Use Rebecca's
existing register from the books — Activation, Ceremony, Practice,
Embodiment, Reset are her words and they work.

**Plans don't have names.** See next section.

## Plans aren't a content type

Earlier drafts of this doc had named curated plans as content — The
Money Plan, The Body Plan, etc. They're out.

The user-facing model is just:

1. **The library** — all practices, all rituals, all journal prompts,
   all readings. Free, browseable, searchable.
2. **The AI plan generator** — builds a personalised plan from the
   user's input. The output is "your plan" — no name, just day 1,
   day 2, etc.

## Library content sources

The Mindset library pulls from three streams. All three feed the
public-facing library; the books and book arcs also feed the AI
generator behind the scenes.

### Stream 1 — Rebecca's books (the spine)

Every tapping script from MONEY, every ritual from MONEY Journal,
every chapter concept from The Money Zone, every day from SLEEP and
WEIGHT LOSS and MANIFESTING — these become library entries. Tapping
scripts become Practice entries (type: TAPPING). Weekly rituals
become Ritual entries. Method chapters become Reading entries.
Journal prompts become JournalPrompt entries. The content is fully
visible, fully searchable, fully Rebecca's voice. Her IP is the
foundation of the library, not a hidden input.

### Stream 2 — Public-domain content + synthesized common-wisdom

The same scaling pattern as Cooking. Many mindset / energy / ritual
sources are public-domain or have content repeated enough across
sources to safely synthesize:

- **EFT (tapping)** — there's substantial public-domain instructional
  material from Gary Craig's original work and decades of derivative
  free content.
- **Meditation & mindfulness** — Buddhist texts, mindfulness teaching
  traditions, breathwork, body-scan practices — much in public
  domain or with permissive reuse.
- **Folk magic / rituals / spells** — bay-leaf manifesting, candle
  magic, sigil work, bath rituals, money rituals — folk traditions
  appear in many public-domain sources.
- **Affirmations / journal prompts** — the underlying patterns are
  not copyrightable; many sources cover similar ground.
- **Visualisation & guided imagery** — long traditions, much public-
  domain or synthesizable.
- **Astrology / moon cycles / seasonal practice** — folk traditions,
  public-domain.

The pattern: where 5+ credible sources cover the same ground, we can
synthesize it in Rebecca's voice. Where content is owned (Amanda
Frances's method, a specific named program), we don't lift it; we
write our own version of the underlying concept if it appears across
public-domain sources too.

### Stream 3 — AI sequencing knowledge (book arcs, not user-visible)

Rebecca's existing book sequences (MONEY: 4-phase 84-day arc; SLEEP:
30-day arc; WEIGHT LOSS: 4-phase 84-day arc; MANIFESTING: 4-phase
84-day arc) feed the AI plan generator as **reference for how to
sequence well** — release-first, then identity, then magnetise, then
anchor (or whatever pattern fits the category). The user doesn't see
"this plan follows MONEY's Phase 2 structure"; she just gets a plan
that's well-sequenced because Rebecca's expertise on sequencing is
baked into the prompt.

### Estimated library entry counts by stream

Per category at full depth, roughly:

- ~30–40% from Rebecca's books (the spine)
- ~50–60% synthesized from public domain
- ~10% Rebecca-original-original (entries she writes specifically for
  the app, beyond the books)

This is the scale that gets Mindset to ~3,000–4,000 entries without
all of it being Rebecca's hand-crafted work.

## The plan generator — free daily pick vs paid custom

Two output modes, gated by tier. The premium boundary is at content
generation: free users get curation from the existing library; paid
users get content generated uniquely for them.

### Free — daily pick

Free users get a featured practice picked for them each day,
surfaced on the Mindset homepage / Today view. No generation, just
curation.

How the pick is chosen:
- Drawn from the existing library
- Matched to the user's declared interests (life-category tags,
  practice-type preferences)
- Influenced by what they've saved, recently used, or marked
  helpful
- Rotated so they don't see the same picks repeatedly

The free user can also browse the whole library, search, save
favourites, mark practices done. The daily pick is the "tell me
what to do today" surface for users who don't want to choose.

It's not a 30-day plan. It's one practice a day, chosen for them.

### Paid — custom plan

Paid users get a personalised 30-day plan where the **content itself
is generated uniquely for them**, not just sequenced from the
library. The library is inspiration / reference for the AI; the
output is fresh.

Entry — two paths, user picks:

**Path A — Pour it out.** Big text box:

> *"Tell me everything. What's on your mind right now? What do you
> want? What's stuck? What's working? What scares you? What feels
> close? Where do you imagine yourself in a year? Pour it all in
> — no order, no editing, no being neat about it."*

Plus optional and small:

- "Anything you don't want me to include?" (text)
- Time per day (defaults to 5–10 min am + pm)

**Path B — Tell me with fields.** A structured form for users who
prefer categories:

- What are you focused on right now? (free text, ~100 words)
- Where are you most stuck? (multi-select tags: money, body,
  business, relationships, motherhood, time, energy, purpose, sleep,
  grief, fear, self-worth, joy, spirituality, health, ageing)
- What's currently working? (free text, optional)
- Time per day (radio, morning + evening separately)
- What practices do you lean toward? (multi-select)
- What do you not want? (anti-tags + free text)

Toggle at the top: "Pour it out" / "Tell me with fields."

What the AI generates for the paid user:

- 30 days of bespoke morning + evening practices written for her
  specific situation. The tapping scripts call out the names and
  specifics from her input. The affirmations use her own goals.
  The journal prompts reference her stuck-on points by name.
- A personalised intro paragraph in Rebecca's voice that names what
  the user said.
- Weekly themes that build over the month.
- Anchor rituals / activities woven into specific days.

This is what justifies the paid tier — content uniquely written for
her, not arranged from a library.

### Behind the scenes

The generator worker:

1. Reads the user's input (text or fields).
2. Reads the library + the book-arc sequencing data + Rebecca's
   voice rules.
3. For each day, generates fresh practices in Rebecca's voice
   targeted at that day's theme in the sequence.
4. Writes the intro paragraph.
5. Saves the result to `UserPlan` + `UserPlanDay` rows. The
   generated practice content lives directly on the PlanDay row
   (not in the library — it's hers, not public).

### Premium gating mechanics

Build everything to free standard per

hard premium architecture upfront; just the flag.

## Voice register — Mindset

(Will move to `docs/mindset-pipeline.md` once locked. Recording here as
the brainstorm reveals it.)

**Good register:**

- Direct and specific.
- Aspirational without apology — Pinterest-board life is a fine goal.
- Wealthy without apology — you can be spiritual *and* want cash and
  assets coming out your hoo-ha.
- Amanda Frances–coded. Florence Given–coded. Rebecca's books'
  register.
- Magical without being culty — spells are OK, "the universe is
  conspiring" is overdone.
- First-person from Rebecca where it lands. Her IP, her voice.
- Edge is allowed. A bit cheeky. A bit playful.

**Anti-tells (cut on sight):**

- AI cadences — "not just X, but Y", "surfaced rather than searched
  for", "permission, not prescription", em-dash appositive pairs.
- Productivity-coaching language — "reset", "tune-up", "challenge",
  "framework", "system".
- Modesty as default — "quiet ambition", "small wins early", "gentle
  progress visibility". Be louder. Be richer. Be wanting more.
- Pseudo-grounded language that smells like an AI trying to be
  grounded — "real talk", "honestly", "look, I get it", "I see you".
- The "you do you" register. Not it.
- "Manifest" as the primary verb of every paragraph. Once in a while,
  yes. Every line, no.
