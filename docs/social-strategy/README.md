# Homemade social-channel strategy

A research-and-synthesis dossier for the bootstrap phase of Homemade's social presence — the period before any designer-teacher contributors, UGC, or paid creators exist. Rebecca and Claude only. Brand-channel content only. No filming Rebecca, no on-camera narration, no recognisable face or voice as the draw.

## What's in here

- `methodology.md` — how the research was conducted, which sources were used, where to treat the findings as solid vs. tentative.
- `research/by-platform/` — what's working per platform: TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest, Instagram, Substack.
- `research/by-niche/` — what's working per niche: cooking, baking, gardening, crochet, knitting.
- `research/account-cards/` — one file per studied account. Handle, niche, platform, follower count, format, what Homemade can take from it.
- `synthesis.md` — patterns that hold across niches and platforms. The compressed read.
- `playbook.md` — the deliverable. Lead-platform recommendation, first-90-days post calendar template, reusable content templates, production stack, voice translation from page to screen, cross-posting strategy, growth milestones.
- `socialblade-worksheet.md` — **required next step before acting on the playbook.** A worksheet listing every studied account with its Social Blade URL. Rebecca populates the Δ-30-day columns; once the growth data is in, we revisit the playbook with currently-growing accounts weighted over historically-large-but-flat ones.

## How to use it

The orchestrator and Rebecca should read in this order:

1. `socialblade-worksheet.md` first. Populate the Δ-30-day columns by browsing Social Blade directly. Until this is filled in, the playbook is acting on follower counts that may be years stale.
2. `playbook.md` second. The actionable output, to be revisited once Social Blade data is in.
3. `synthesis.md` for the reasoning behind the playbook's choices.
4. Anything in `research/` only if a specific question needs sourcing.

Account cards are reference material — open one when sizing up a competitor or studying a format.

## What this is not

- A list of personality-led influencers to follow or partner with.
- A plan that requires Rebecca on camera. That sits in a later phase of the brand, once an audience exists and a face can be added deliberately.
- A paid-channel strategy. No ads, no sponsorships, no creator collaborations at this stage.
- A copy bank. The playbook contains templates and worked examples; the bot-as-author pipeline produces the actual posts when the time comes.

## Status

Drafted in one research session. The shortlist of accounts is calibrated for replicability by a two-person team using AI illustration, off-the-shelf video editors, and the brand voice. **Follower counts in this dossier come from marketing-blog aggregators and may be years stale.** Before acting on the playbook, the Social Blade worksheet must be populated so we're acting on current growth velocity, not historical scale.
