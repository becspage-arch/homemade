# Crochet

## State of the niche

Crochet is the most personality-led of the textile niches on short-form video and the most brand-channel-friendly on long-form tutorial content. The TikTok feed is dominated by named makers who post finished-object reveals, transformation videos, and styling moments. The YouTube space, by contrast, is overwhelmingly hands-only stitch tutorials where the format is reproducible.

Pinterest is enormous for free patterns and works as a long-tail traffic engine for written patterns hosted on a publisher's site.

The audience demographics overlap heavily with Homemade's stated audience: women, 30–55, who took up the craft during or after the 2020 surge and who continue to seek pattern depth and technique clarity.

## What's working for non-personality-led accounts

**Bella Coco** (Sarah-Jayne) on YouTube — 1.9M subscribers. Hands-and-yarn camera framing with voice-over narration. The face occasionally appears but the format is dominated by stitches and yarn. The closest replicable model for a Homemade crochet YouTube presence.

**The Crochet Crowd** (Mikey Sellick) on YouTube — 1.27M subscribers. More personality-led than Bella Coco. Useful as a comparison point but not the format Homemade should imitate.

**MilArt Marroquin** on YouTube — 1.16M subscribers. Spanish-language follow-along tutorials. The visible stitches transcend language.

**Lidia Crochet Tricot** on YouTube — over 1M subscribers, multi-technique (hooks, needles, Tunisian).

**Naztazia** (Donna Wolfe) on YouTube — 2.3M subscribers, multi-craft. Faceless and hands-led for the most part.

**TejiendodeCorazon** on YouTube — Spanish-speaking, hands-only stitch combinations.

**Lalylaland** on Instagram — 160K followers. Amigurumi and decorative-crochet brand-channel. Process photography and finished-object styling.

**Allfreecrochet** and **Nicki's Homemade Crafts** on Pinterest — pattern aggregators with established Pinterest authority and free-pattern conversion funnels.

## What Homemade can do that the field can't

The crochet world on YouTube is dominated by hands-on-camera narration with relatively casual production. The brand-direction-grade aesthetic (natural light, linen and wood surfaces, the Homemade palette, careful styling) is not the dominant visual register in crochet tutorials. There's room for a publication-quality crochet channel that treats stitch tutorials with the same visual seriousness as a Peaceful Cuisine baking video.

The depth angle also applies. The Homemade voice rules can produce pattern instructions that read as literary as Nigel Slater's recipes (specific, warm, precisely worded) in a category where most pattern writing reads as engineering.

## Sub-niches with the strongest brand fit

In rough priority order:

1. **Granny squares and motif work.** Reference HTML on the Homemade plan already covers this — it's the template tutorial design. High Pinterest traffic, evergreen content.
2. **Blanket and homeware patterns.** Throws, cushions, table runners. Aesthetic alignment with the Homemade brand direction is direct.
3. **Wearables — cardigans, jumpers, wraps.** Slower content cycle but high engagement when a pattern lands well.
4. **Amigurumi.** Massive on TikTok (27.3M+ hashtag posts). The visual is bright and toy-like, which is slightly off-brand for Homemade — handle with the Homemade aesthetic restraint or skip in the launch period.
5. **Stitch dictionaries.** Reference content. Strong Pinterest and YouTube saves rate.

## Recommended lead platforms for crochet

YouTube for tutorial depth. Pinterest for pattern traffic. Instagram for finished-object photography and Reels of process work. TikTok is the lower-priority platform for crochet specifically — the format dominance there is personality-driven and harder for a brand channel to break into.
