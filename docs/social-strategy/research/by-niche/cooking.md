# Cooking

## State of the niche

Cooking is the densest, most competitive, and most accessible niche of the five for non-personality-led content. The overhead recipe demo has been the universal video grammar since 2018 and shows no sign of being displaced. The skill ceiling for entry is low: anyone with a phone and a window for natural light can make a competent first video. But the skill ceiling for staying watchable is high. Production polish, sound design, and recipe selection separate the channels at one million subscribers from the channels at fifty thousand.

The audience surplus is enormous. There is no shortage of viewers for well-made faceless cooking content. The bottleneck is the producer's editorial standard, which is the place where Homemade's brand discipline becomes the unfair advantage.

## What's working for non-personality-led accounts

**On YouTube:** the Peaceful Cuisine / Cooking Tree meditative-overhead format dominates the high-end aesthetic register. 2.47M and 5.2M subscribers respectively, no face, no voice, no music. The format is replicable and the English-language opportunity is wide open — Cooking Tree is Korean and Peaceful Cuisine is Japanese.

**On TikTok:** hands-only and overhead recipe demos work at all scales. Le Marais Bakery (~349K) turned a bakery production line into 110M+ views. Ikneadbread (189K) built a sourdough-scoring audience without ever showing a face. The format is the same across followers ranges; the difference is recipe selection and styling.

**On Pinterest:** brand-channel publishers (Food52, NYT Cooking, BBC Good Food) dominate the food-search surface. Each tutorial published on homemade.education can produce 5–10 pins that earn long-tail traffic for years.

**On Instagram:** the sourdough niche (shebakesourdough 2M, amybakesbread 858K) is the strongest non-personality-led sub-category. Bread Instagram is a self-contained world with established conventions that Homemade can step into.

**On Substack:** personality-driven. The opportunity for Homemade is to build an editorial-voice newsletter that doesn't depend on a named writer.

## What Homemade can do that the field can't

The dominant English-language Peaceful-Cuisine analogue does not yet exist. The Japanese and Korean originators have huge global audiences but their content is paced and styled for an East Asian visual tradition. A British-rooted, Monty-Don / Nigel-Slater-voiced, English-country / Scandi-restraint version of the same format is an open lane.

Homemade's content depth is also a meaningful structural advantage. Most cooking creators have neither the time nor the inclination to write a 3,000-word tutorial under each video. Homemade has the tutorial first and the video as the entry point. That asymmetry shows up in the saves rate, the email signup rate, and the long-tail SEO.

## Sub-niches with the strongest brand fit

In rough priority order for the launch period:

1. **Bread and sourdough.** Visually rich. The audience is engaged and self-identified. The Homemade visual language (linen, ceramic, sage tones, warm light) is the natural styling for bread.
2. **Preserves, jams, ferments.** Strong on Pinterest and Substack particularly. Process-led — beautiful glass jars, hands stirring, slow change over time.
3. **Stocks, soups, mother sauces.** Technique foundation content. Doesn't trend on TikTok at the level bread does, but ranks well on YouTube search and earns repeat saves on Pinterest.
4. **Pastry.** High aesthetic ceiling. Croissant lamination is a TikTok native (Le Marais Bakery is the proof). Choux, shortcrust, hot-water, filo all work in overhead format.
5. **Confectionery, dairy, charcuterie.** Niche but high-engagement. The audience that wants to make their own butter or marshmallow is small and committed.

## Sub-niches to skip in the launch period

- Fast / midweek dinners. The category is saturated with personality creators and the brand-fit is poor (slow living, not weeknight rush).
- Restaurant recreations. Off-brand for Homemade and dominated by personalities (Joshua Weissman, Mythical Kitchen).
- Diet-led content (keto, carnivore, plant-based-as-identity). Off-brand.

## Recommended lead platforms for cooking

YouTube long-form for the depth play. TikTok and Instagram Reels for discovery. Pinterest for evergreen traffic. Substack once a weekly letter cadence is sustainable.
