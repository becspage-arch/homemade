# Knitting

## State of the niche

Knitting is the sparsest niche in this research set for non-personality-led content. The major knitting creators run podcasts (the historic format that dominated YouTube knitting from 2010 onwards), vlogs, or personality-led Instagram presences. Even the pattern-house brand channels (PetiteKnit, Brooklyn Tweed) sit alongside the named designer rather than independently of them.

Two structural reasons. First, knitting is slower than crochet: a finished garment can take months, so creator content tends to be project-diary rather than per-tutorial. Second, the knitting audience values the long-running relationship with a particular designer or teacher, in a way the cooking and gardening audiences do not.

This shapes the strategy. Homemade's knitting content should lean on pattern and technique depth rather than trying to break into the social conversation. The lead platforms for knitting are Pinterest and YouTube, with Instagram as a secondary surface and TikTok deprioritised.

## What's working for non-personality-led accounts

**PetiteKnit** (Mette Wendelboe Okkels) on Instagram — ~1.3M followers. Danish pattern house. The garments are the subject; the designer is named but rarely the focus. Reels of garments being worn, close-ups of stitches and yarn. The strongest brand-channel knitting Instagram template.

**Brooklyn Tweed** (Jared Flood) on Instagram — ~175K followers. American yarn and pattern brand. Hands, yarn, swatches, garments on bodies. Pure brand channel.

**Lion Brand Yarn** on Pinterest — the largest yarn-brand Pinterest presence. The pattern-search-engine play. Free patterns indexed across hundreds of board collections.

**Deemo Crafts** on YouTube — faceless knitting and crocheting videos. Smaller scale but the format is the most directly replicable.

**Knit Together** (Anne Vally) on Substack — thousands of subscribers, weekly slow-knitting letter. Personality-led byline but tonally the closest analogue to where Homemade's knitting Substack content should sit.

**Cherry Heart** (Sandra Paul) on Substack — thousands of subscribers, multi-craft (crochet, knitting, sewing).

## What Homemade can do that the field can't

The knitting field has not been served by a publication-grade, illustration-led, depth-focused brand channel in English. PetiteKnit comes closest in the Scandi register but the brand sells patterns rather than teaching the craft. Brooklyn Tweed is yarn-led. Neither operates as a free-tutorial publication of the depth Homemade is building.

The illustration-first visual strategy is particularly suited to knitting. Stitch diagrams, schematic drawings, fit measurements, and gauge swatches all illustrate beautifully and work better as drawn references than as photographs. The visual register fits Homemade's brand direction directly.

## Sub-niches with the strongest brand fit

In rough priority order:

1. **Stitch dictionaries.** Reference content. Highest Pinterest saves rate of any knitting category. Illustrates well.
2. **Garment construction techniques — top-down raglans, seamless yokes, buttonbands.** Technique tutorials with diagrams. Strong YouTube search intent.
3. **Beginner pattern series — first scarf, first jumper, first sock.** The Homemade beginner-mode toggle (already shipped in Phase 4) is the structural fit for this. Pattern + glossary expansion + substitutions field is exactly what new knitters need.
4. **Cables, lace, colourwork.** Higher-skill technique content. Smaller audience but more engaged.
5. **Yarn and fibre education — wool breeds, plant fibres, gauge, blocking.** Reference content. Substack workhorse.

## Sub-niches to skip in the launch period

- Sock knitting in the round. Hyper-specific niche; smaller TikTok / Pinterest audience than the wearable garments.
- Toy knitting. Off-brand for the Homemade aesthetic.

## Recommended lead platforms for knitting

Pinterest for evergreen pattern traffic. YouTube for technique tutorials with diagrams and overlays. Instagram for finished-garment Reels and stitch close-ups. Substack for the weekly slow-knitting letter once the audience cadence supports it. TikTok deprioritised — the format dominance there is podcast-and-personality, with no clear brand-channel pattern to imitate at scale.

## Flag for the orchestrator

Knitting is the niche where the strict "no personality" stance is most costly. The non-personality-led ceiling here is lower than in cooking or gardening. When Homemade eventually adds creator contributions in Phase 6, knitting is the niche that will benefit fastest from named human teachers — patterns-and-yarn brand-channels carry an audience to about 200K–1.3M; adding a named designer voice often unlocks the next order of magnitude.
