# Gardening

## State of the niche

Gardening is the most asymmetric niche across platforms. TikTok and YouTube have thriving non-personality-led accounts working with overhead time-lapse, garden-walk b-roll, and educational text overlays. Instagram, by contrast, is heavily personality-led — particularly in the UK where the Chelsea Flower Show and RHS ecosystem promotes named designers and broadcasters. Pinterest is excellent for evergreen "what to plant when" content. Substack has a small but engaged audience for long-form garden writing in the Monty Don / Vita Sackville-West register.

The niche is also seasonal in a way the others are not. Sowing schedules, harvest windows, frost dates, and bloom times structure the content calendar. Homemade's gardening content should be built around the British growing year from the start.

## What's working for non-personality-led accounts

**Vegetablegarden02** ("Horta") on TikTok — ~1.1M followers, fully faceless vegetable-garden content. The proof of the format at scale.

**Cannidiggit** ("The Vegetable Gardener") on TikTok — faceless kitchen-garden content.

**Marf's Kitchen Garden** — 478K TikTok and 215K Instagram. Brand-channel format, landscape-led aesthetic, London-based. The author has a name and a book but the content reads as garden-led, not face-led.

**GrowVeg** (Ben Vanheems) on YouTube — 623K subscribers. Tutorial format, illustrated overlays, hands and garden footage. The strongest brand-channel YouTube template.

**Charles Dowding** on YouTube — 640–656K subscribers. On-camera but the format (slow garden walk, technique-led voice-over, hands in the soil) is structurally replicable with a different narrator. The single-strongest reference for what Homemade's gardening voice should sound like in audio.

**The Gardening Mind** (Jo Thompson) on Substack — tens of thousands of subscribers in 165 countries. The strongest brand-channel-adjacent Substack in gardening.

**Wild Way** (Jack Wallington) on Substack — thousands of subscribers, advanced gardening, wildflower communities, edible cultivation. Yorkshire-based, lyrical writing. Closest voice analogue to Homemade's gardening register.

## What Homemade can do that the field can't

Gardening content on social platforms tends to be either:

(a) Personality-led, with the gardener as the draw. Or
(b) Brand-channel but generic, with photo libraries that look the same on every gardening account.

Homemade has a third option: a brand-channel gardening presence with the visual standard of the personality accounts (illustrated diagrams in the Homemade style, seasonal charts, British growing-year specificity) and the trust signal of a publisher rather than a personality. That position is not currently occupied.

The British seasonal angle is a particular structural advantage. Most large gardening accounts are US-based with US growing zones, which UK readers have to mentally translate. A UK-rooted gardening publication with British sowing dates, RHS-aware variety recommendations, and British weather patterns has a defensible position.

## Sub-niches with the strongest brand fit

In rough priority order:

1. **Kitchen garden / allotment vegetables.** Largest TikTok and YouTube audience. Tomatoes, salad leaves, beans, courgettes, alliums.
2. **Companion planting, succession sowing, sowing calendars.** Diagram and chart content — Pinterest gold, Substack workhorses.
3. **Soft fruit — strawberries, raspberries, currants.** Visually rich, seasonal, photogenic.
4. **Herbs.** Year-round content. Window-sill scope makes it accessible to renting readers without outdoor space.
5. **Cut flowers — dahlias, sweet peas, sunflowers.** Aesthetic strength. Crosses cleanly into the slow-living lifestyle audience.

## Sub-niches to handle carefully

- **Houseplants.** Huge audience but the aesthetic (bright, glossy, monstera-and-pothos) is at odds with Homemade's English-country register. Better positioned as a future sub-category than a launch focus.
- **Garden design.** High prestige in the UK but heavily dominated by named designers (Tom Stuart-Smith, Charlie Harpur, Jo Thompson). Difficult to enter as a brand channel without a clear point of view.

## Recommended lead platforms for gardening

YouTube for technique tutorials and garden walks. Pinterest for seasonal charts and pin-driven traffic. TikTok for time-lapse and overhead garden b-roll. Substack for the long-form Monty-Don-voiced essay (medium term). Instagram is the secondary surface, not the lead, because the UK gardening Instagram ecosystem is personality-saturated.
