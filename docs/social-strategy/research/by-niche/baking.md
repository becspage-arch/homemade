# Baking

## State of the niche

Baking sits adjacent to cooking but has its own discovery surface, its own creator pool, and its own audience conventions. The faceless format is even more native here than in cooking — the dough, the bread, the cake is genuinely the protagonist. Many of the largest baking accounts on every platform run as faceless or near-faceless brand channels by default.

Pastry, sourdough and cake decorating are the three sub-niches that generate the most engagement. Bread (particularly sourdough) is the largest single content cluster.

## What's working for non-personality-led accounts

**Cooking Tree** (5.2M YouTube subscribers) is technically a baking channel — Korean desserts and café drinks. The closest-to-perfect format match for Homemade's brand register lives in the baking-not-savoury world.

**Le Marais Bakery** (~349K TikTok) made faceless production-line content the foundation of a nationally-shipping business.

**Ikneadbread** (~189K TikTok) demonstrates that decorative sourdough scoring alone is a sustainable content niche.

**She Bakes Sourdough** (~2M Instagram), **Amy Bakes Bread** (~858K), **Everything Sourdough** (~381K), **Sourdough Explained** (~379K) — the sourdough Instagram cluster is the largest non-personality-led content cluster in the entire baking landscape.

**King Arthur Baking** is the structural template: a heritage brand-channel account that posts recipes, technique videos, and ingredient information across TikTok (~144K), Instagram (large), Pinterest, and YouTube without leaning on a personality.

**Nicola Lamb's Kitchen Projects** is the strongest personality-led Substack in the British baking space, included here only as a voice reference. The depth and editorial standard she sets is the bar for what good baking writing looks like.

## What Homemade can do that the field can't

The non-personality-led baking world is dominated by sourdough. Cake, pastry, and confectionery have proportionally fewer brand-channel exemplars. The sourdough crowd has been over-served; the laminated-pastry crowd, the choux crowd, the British classic-bakes crowd, the marshmallow-and-fudge crowd have not. Homemade can pick the under-served sub-niches and build position there before adding the bread coverage.

## Sub-niches with the strongest brand fit

In rough priority order:

1. **Sourdough and other yeasted breads.** Largest audience, most established conventions. Homemade can step in with depth (24-hour timeline, common failure modes, scoring patterns from public-domain references) and competitive videos.
2. **Pastry — croissants, puff, shortcrust, choux.** Highest aesthetic ceiling. Lamination is TikTok-native. Choux is photogenic on Pinterest.
3. **Cakes — British classics, layer cakes, traybakes.** Strong on Pinterest. Decorative cakes work on TikTok overhead.
4. **Confectionery — caramel, fudge, marshmallow, nougat, brittle.** Niche, high engagement when the photography is right.
5. **Biscuits, scones, and small bakes.** Workhorse content for Pinterest and Substack. Lower TikTok and YouTube ceiling but strong long-tail.

## Voice translation notes

The baking voice register on social platforms tends to be more technical than the cooking register: measurements, hydration percentages, temperatures, times. The Homemade brand voice (Alice Waters / Nigel Slater warmth) softens this without losing precision. The model is: "75% hydration, fermented overnight in a cool kitchen" rather than "BAKERS REJOICE: Easy 75% hydration sourdough!"

Captions and descriptions for baking content should always include:

- Measurements in metric first, imperial in brackets.
- Temperature in °C and °F.
- Hydration percentage if relevant.
- One specific timing detail (the only place machine durations are fine).

## Recommended lead platforms for baking

YouTube long-form (Cooking Tree analogue). Instagram Reels for the sourdough audience. TikTok for cake decoration and pastry process. Pinterest for evergreen recipe traffic.
