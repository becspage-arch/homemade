# The Gardening Mind

- **Handle:** jothompson.substack.com
- **Creator:** Jo Thompson (UK garden designer)
- **Primary platform:** Substack
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Gardening / design and small-garden teaching

## Scale and trajectory

- Substack: tens of thousands of subscribers across 165 countries.
- Personality-led (Jo is the byline) but the strategy is exportable to a brand-channel voice.

## Format

Weekly long-form letter. Planting tutorials, courses (Small Garden Design), private chat thread where members share photos of their gardens, problem-solve, and identify plants. Free tier serves the letter; paid tier unlocks the course and the community.

The writing voice sits in the educated-British-designer register — Vita Sackville-West and Monty Don territory, with a contemporary clarity.

## Engagement signals

The 165-country geographic spread is the unusual signal. Garden writing in the Vita / Monty register has an international audience even when the planting advice is British-specific.

## Standout patterns

- Free letter as the front door; paid course and membership monetise.
- Community chat thread is the retention loop — paying members stay for the conversation as much as the content.
- Cross-recommendations with adjacent Substack writers drive ongoing growth.

## What Homemade can take

- **The audience for British-rooted gardening writing is global.** This matters for Homemade's eventual reach beyond the UK.
- **The free-letter-plus-paid-membership model.** Homemade can run a free weekly letter as the Substack front door, with the eventual premium tier offering deeper content (full course series, community, archive) on the homemade.education side.
- **Cross-recommendations are the Substack growth engine.** Building relationships with adjacent slow-living, food, and craft newsletters is the highest-ROI activity on the platform.
- **A garden-design point of view is a clear positioning.** Even on Homemade's broader scope, the gardening sub-section can have a strong opinion — what gardens we love, what we don't.

## What we'd do differently

- Editorial-voice byline rather than personal byline. Homemade's letter would be signed "Homemade" or with a section editor's name, not Rebecca's.
- The tone discipline of the brand voice rules. Jo's writing is excellent but slightly more designer-individual; Homemade's voice references skew slightly more populist (Monty Don rather than Vita) for the audience demographic.
