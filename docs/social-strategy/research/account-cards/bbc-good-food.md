# BBC Good Food

- **Handle:** @bbcgoodfood (Instagram, TikTok), bbcgoodfood (Pinterest)
- **Owner:** Immediate Media
- **Primary platform:** Multi-platform; site bbcgoodfood.com is the centre.
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Cooking / brand-channel publisher (UK-rooted)

## Scale and trajectory

- Instagram: ~1.4M followers.
- Pinterest: ~45K followers (older reporting), described as the brand's "biggest social referrer because of the brand's very visual content."
- Website: monthly audience grew 6% in 2022; the site reaches roughly 25% of UK internet users.
- The most directly comparable UK brand-channel publisher in this research set.

## Format

Multi-format brand-channel.

- **Instagram:** Reels, recipe carousels, finished-dish photographs. Brand-led; no recurring presenter.
- **TikTok:** Short recipe demos, overhead and hands-only.
- **Pinterest:** High-volume recipe pins.
- **Website:** the recipe library; the social channels distribute back to it.

The visual style across BBC Good Food is photographic (bright, food-styled, slightly traditional) and the audience expects approachable family-meal-oriented content.

## Engagement signals

The Pinterest-as-biggest-referrer signal is the relevant one for Homemade. Visual brands earn meaningful traffic from Pinterest even with relatively modest follower counts. The pin design and the recipe library carry the traffic, not the social-feed engagement.

## Standout patterns

- The recipe pin with the dish name + key descriptor ("easy chocolate cake," "midweek pasta"). SEO-conscious naming.
- Heavy seasonal cadence — Christmas content from October, summer salads from May.
- Recipe collections (boards) by occasion ("Sunday Roast," "Christmas Dinner," "Baby-Led Weaning") that earn long-term traffic.

## What Homemade can take

- **A UK brand-channel publisher can reach 1.4M Instagram followers.** This is the directly-comparable scale ceiling for an editorial cooking brand without a single celebrity presenter.
- **Pinterest as referrer machine.** BBC Good Food's "biggest social referrer" observation generalises: Pinterest will likely be Homemade's biggest traffic source from social once the pin library reaches scale.
- **Seasonal cadence sells.** Aligning the content calendar to the British year (Christmas pudding in October, Easter eggs in February, summer salads in May) is the durable strategy.

## What we'd do differently

- Aesthetic register. BBC Good Food leans slightly traditional and bright; Homemade is restrained, English-country-plus-Scandi, illustration-led. The Homemade visual register should be visibly more distinctive on a feed where BBC Good Food's pins blend in.
- Slow-living positioning rather than midweek-family-meals. BBC Good Food serves a broader UK audience including weekday-rush readers; Homemade serves a narrower, slower audience.
