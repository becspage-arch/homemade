# Le Marais Bakery

- **Handle:** @lemaraisbakery
- **Primary platform:** TikTok
- **Cross-platform:** Instagram (~110K followers), lemaraisbakery.com (brick-and-mortar bakery in San Francisco)
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Baking / French viennoiserie (croissants, pain au chocolat, cinnamon rolls)

## Scale and trajectory

- TikTok: ~347–349K followers. Since January 2020, videos have accumulated 110M+ views.
- The TikTok presence has turned a single San Francisco bakery into a national shipping business.
- Borderline classification: the bakery owner exists and is named on the brand website but the TikTok content is overwhelmingly faceless process footage. Treat as brand-channel format.

## Format

Overhead and side-angle shots of bakery production. Hands shaping, folding, glazing, scoring, slicing. Bakery aprons and stainless-steel worktops visible but the faces of bakers are cut out of frame. Sound design: bakery ambient (mixers, oven doors, dough slap) plus quiet music tracks.

Posts are short (typically 15–45 seconds) and focus on a single product moment: laminating croissant dough, rolling pain au chocolat, glazing a finished pastry tray.

## Engagement signals

Bakery TikTok ("croissanttok," "bakerytok") communities engage heavily. Strong saves and shares. Frequent reuse of viral sounds adapted to bakery rhythms.

## Standout video patterns

- The "stack" video — a tray of identical pastries appearing one after another in rapid cuts. Aesthetic but also a flex (production volume).
- The "lamination reveal" — slow folding then a clean cross-section cut.
- The "tray going into the oven" — fixed shot of the oven door closing, then time-lapse to opening with finished product.
- Frequent reposting of the same product made in different conditions (different days, different bakers).

## What Homemade can take

- **A working bakery is a content engine.** Homemade doesn't have a physical bakery, but the principle (the production process is the content) translates to the home kitchen if the staging is consistent.
- **Faceless production-line as a category.** Homemade's "watch the dough being made" content can borrow the framing directly: overhead, hands-only, ambient sound, short.
- **TikTok-native posting cadence.** Le Marais Bakery posts frequently (multiple times per week, often daily during product launches). The format is short enough to be sustainable.
- **The brand association loop.** TikTok views → bakery awareness → online shipping orders. For Homemade the loop is TikTok views → tutorial site traffic → email list → bookmarked tutorials.

## What we'd do differently

- More instructional framing. Le Marais Bakery doesn't teach — it shows. Homemade's content needs the teach-the-craft step (overlay text with the technique name, the temperature, the timing) because the audience reads as well as watches.
- The brand identity is implicit at Le Marais — bakery uniforms, signage in frame. Homemade's brand identity should be in the worktop, palette, props, and serif overlay rather than uniforms.
