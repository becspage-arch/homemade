# Peaceful Cuisine

- **Handle:** @peaceful_cuisine
- **Creator:** Ryoya Takashima
- **Primary platform:** YouTube — [Peaceful Cuisine channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCQBG3PzyQKY8ieMG2gDAyOQ)
- **Cross-platform:** Instagram @peaceful_cuisine, personal website ryoyatakashima.com
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Cooking / plant-based, meditative

## Scale and trajectory

- YouTube: ~2.47M subscribers, ~166M total views as of recent reporting. Channel started 2010.
- Growth has been steady rather than explosive. The channel compounded for over a decade and remains active. Verify live: https://socialblade.com/youtube/c/peacefulcuisine

## Format

Overhead camera, fixed. No face. No voice-over. No music. The audio is the cooking itself: knife on board, butter sizzling, batter folding. Each video runs 8–15 minutes. Plant-based recipes — bread, desserts, vegetable mains, tofu dishes. Minimal text overlay; on-screen recipe instructions are sparse and quiet.

The kitchen is consistent: warm wood worktop, simple linen, ceramic vessels, soft daylight. The visual identity is the brand more reliably than any logo.

## Engagement signals

Watch-time-led growth. Comment sections are warm and contemplative rather than viral — viewers describe playing the videos as ambient sound while working or sleeping. Long-tail re-watching is the platform mechanic that compounds.

## Standout video patterns

- "How to make X" titles that read as cookbook chapter headings: clean, specific, no clickbait.
- The first 5–10 seconds show the finished dish, then a clean cut to the first ingredient.
- Recipe is summarised in a static frame at the end with all measurements; no spoken outro.

## What Homemade can take

- **The format is exportable.** Calm overhead + no voice + no music + visible sound design is now a proven multi-million-subscriber YouTube category. There is no English-language equivalent built for the Homemade audience.
- **Decade-long compounding.** Peaceful Cuisine is the proof that a slow, consistent, faceless channel can outgrow personality-led contemporaries given enough years.
- **Identity is the kitchen, not the creator.** Homemade's locked palette and brand direction can produce the same effect with a single consistent worktop, prop set, and lighting setup.
- **Recipe selection.** Plant-based isn't the model to copy; the model to copy is depth over novelty. Each Peaceful Cuisine recipe is a category landmark, not a "ten unusual sourdough hacks" listicle.

## What we'd do differently

- British produce and seasonality (tomatoes in August, sourdough in winter, preserves in autumn). The Japanese-aesthetic minimalism translates but the ingredient calendar should be local.
- Slightly warmer styling — the Peaceful Cuisine kitchen reads cool and zen-Japanese. Homemade's English-country / Scandi blend should read warmer (linen napkins, terracotta accents, sage tones).
- A short generic narrative voice-over, occasionally — Peaceful Cuisine uses none, but a Nigel-Slater-voiced one-sentence intro on certain tutorials would suit the Homemade voice direction.
