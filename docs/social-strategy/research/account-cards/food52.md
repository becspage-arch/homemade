# Food52

- **Handle:** @food52
- **Primary platform:** Pinterest (and food52.com)
- **Cross-platform:** Instagram (large), TikTok, YouTube, email newsletter, e-commerce shop
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Cooking / brand-channel publisher

## Scale and trajectory

- Pinterest: ~288K followers, 10M+ monthly viewers across 100+ boards.
- Founded 2009 by Amanda Hesser and Merrill Stubbs (former NYT food). Brand-channel format, no single recurring presenter on the surface.
- The closest commercial-scale parallel to what Homemade is structurally — a publisher with deep recipe content, a community, and a product business.

## Format

Pinterest pin format: single high-quality recipe photograph, overlay title in a serif, link to the recipe page on food52.com. Boards organised by recipe type ("Breakfast and Brunch," "Soups," "Desserts"), occasion ("Holiday Baking," "Thanksgiving"), ingredient ("Fall Vegetables," "Citrus"), and theme.

Content originates as articles on food52.com — recipes, technique pieces, ingredient guides, kitchen-product reviews. The Pinterest layer is the distribution surface, not the editorial centre.

## Engagement signals

Pinterest monthly viewers are the more meaningful metric than follower count. 10M+ monthly viewers means roughly that many people see Food52 pins per month across feeds and search. The follower-to-monthly-viewer ratio (288K : 10M+) demonstrates the platform's pin-search-driven economics — most engagement comes from non-followers discovering pins.

## Standout pin patterns

- Single dominant photograph; minimal compositing.
- Title in a serif overlay at the top or bottom, never centred over the photograph's main subject.
- A short keyword-rich description (50–150 characters) with the recipe name in the first sentence.
- Cross-boarding — the same pin appears on multiple relevant boards over weeks.

## What Homemade can take

- **The publisher-to-Pinterest funnel.** Every tutorial on homemade.education should produce multiple pins. Food52 does roughly 5–10 pins per recipe; Homemade can match this from the start.
- **Board structure as a long-term asset.** Food52's boards have been compounding since 2014. The earliest pins still earn impressions. A Homemade board for "Sourdough" started in year one earns traffic in year five.
- **The community + product layer.** Food52 has a discussion community (Hotline), reviews, contests, and a shop. Homemade's roadmap (Phase 5 UGC + Phase 7 marketplace) is structurally similar — Food52 is the proof that the model scales.
- **Brand consistency in pin design.** Every Food52 pin reads as Food52 even without the watermark. The photography style, type treatment, and composition discipline are the brand. Homemade's locked visual identity gives the same potential.

## What we'd do differently

- The Homemade audience is narrower than Food52's (slow-living English-country-Scandi vs. broad American food media). Pin selection should lean into the brand voice and aesthetic rather than mirror Food52's range.
- Illustration alongside photography. Food52 is photo-led; Homemade is illustration-first. Pins with the Homemade illustration style are a differentiator on a feed otherwise dominated by photographs.
