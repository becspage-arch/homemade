# Cooking Tree (쿠킹트리)

- **Handle:** Cooking tree 쿠킹트리
- **Primary platform:** YouTube — [Cooking Tree channel](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCtby6rJtBGgUm-2oD_E7bzw)
- **Cross-platform:** Instagram presence; the YouTube channel is the centre of gravity.
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Baking / Korean desserts and café drinks

## Scale and trajectory

- YouTube: ~5.2M subscribers. Sustained growth over multiple years.
- The Korean "healing cooking" (힐링 쿠킹) sub-genre was pioneered by this channel — bakeries in Korea now market products as "Cooking tree style." That's a brand-defining-category position rather than just a channel.
- Verify live: https://socialblade.com/youtube/c/cookingtree

## Format

Pristine white kitchen. Precise overhead shots. No face, no voice, no music. ASMR sound design carries the rhythm: egg cracking, batter folding, chocolate melting, cream piping, glass scraping. Each video runs 5–10 minutes. Content is overwhelmingly desserts and café drinks — cakes, tarts, mousses, cookies, beverages.

Text on screen carries ingredient names and quantities, in white serif type, minimally placed.

## Engagement signals

ASMR community overlap drives strong cross-discovery. Videos are routinely shared as ambient background — in cafés, study rooms, sleep playlists. This produces unusually high watch-completion rates for the length.

## Standout video patterns

- Tile shot of finished dessert as the thumbnail. Pinterest-perfect framing, fully styled.
- Title is just the recipe name. "Strawberry Mousse Cake" — nothing else. No exclamation, no superlative.
- First frame is the finished thing, then immediate cut to ingredient placement.
- All measurements in grams and millilitres, displayed cleanly.

## What Homemade can take

- **The genre-defining play.** Cooking Tree didn't enter a category; it created one. A British / English-country equivalent (slow, deep, restrained baking with the Homemade visual identity) could occupy a similar position.
- **White-kitchen consistency is the brand.** Homemade can replicate this with a single styled worktop area, the same props rotated, the same daylight angle. No on-screen branding needed; the consistency of the staging IS the brand.
- **Pure ASMR sound design.** No music, no voice. The cracking, folding, pouring sounds are the audio. This is the lowest-skill, highest-aesthetic audio strategy available.
- **Café-friendly content.** Recipes that translate well to the home kitchen but feel like a café treat. Tartlets, layered desserts, pretty beverages.

## What we'd do differently

- Less white-on-white. The Cooking Tree visual register is Scandinavian-Japanese-minimalist; Homemade's is English-country plus Scandi. Warm linen, terracotta accents, walnut wood would shift the palette without losing the calm.
- British baking canon. Cooking Tree leans Korean / international café aesthetic. Homemade's content should pull from British baking (puddings, scones, fruit cakes, traybakes) alongside the universal European patisserie.
- A spare voice-over option for instructional clarity. Cooking Tree uses none; the Homemade audience expects a tutorial they can follow, so on-screen instructions need to be more explicit even if no voice is used.
