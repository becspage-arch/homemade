# Wild Way

- **Handle:** wildway.info (Substack)
- **Creator:** Jack Wallington (UK garden writer, Yorkshire-based)
- **Primary platform:** Substack
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Gardening / wildflower communities, edible cultivation, advanced technique

## Scale and trajectory

- Substack: thousands of subscribers.
- The closest voice analogue to where Homemade's gardening Substack content should sit — lyrical, observant, British, with a Yorkshire-and-Vita-Sackville-West sensibility.

## Format

Long-form weekly or fortnightly letters. Topics range over wildflower meadows, advanced vegetable cultivation, climate-adapted planting, and the writer's own exposed Yorkshire garden. Writing is unhurried and specific — the kind of paragraph that names a particular cultivar and explains why it works in that location.

Photographs are competent but not styled; the writing carries.

## Engagement signals

Small but devoted subscriber base. Open rates and comment engagement run high for a Substack of this size. Cross-recommended by other British garden writers, which is the Substack growth flywheel.

## Standout patterns

- The "exposed garden" angle. Jack writes from a specific real garden in a specific real climate, and the writing's authority comes from that situated knowledge.
- Wildflower plant communities as a recurring theme — a specific lens that organises broad content.
- Edible cultivation alongside ornamental — the slow-living food-and-garden crossover that suits the Homemade audience precisely.

## What Homemade can take

- **The literary voice register is reachable in a brand channel.** Wild Way's writing is the bar. The Homemade voice rules and reference writers (Vita Sackville-West, Monty Don) can produce paragraphs in the same register, written editorially rather than personally.
- **Place-specific authority.** Homemade's gardening content has the same opportunity to be visibly British — Yorkshire dales, Somerset hedgerows, Cotswolds kitchen gardens. The audience reads the specificity as expertise.
- **Edible / ornamental crossover.** This sub-positioning suits Homemade's broader content scope (cooking and gardening are adjacent sections of the site) better than a pure ornamental or pure vegetable focus.

## What we'd do differently

- The byline-as-brand. Homemade's Substack carries an editorial voice not a personal one. The writing register is the same; the signature changes.
- Photography styled to the brand direction. Wild Way's photos are documentary; Homemade's would be styled.
