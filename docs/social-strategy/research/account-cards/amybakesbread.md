# Amy Bakes Bread

- **Handle:** @amybakesbread
- **Creator:** Amy Coyne
- **Primary platform:** Instagram
- **Cross-platform:** TikTok, website (sourdough resource library)
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Baking / sourdough recipes and troubleshooting

## Scale and trajectory

- Instagram: ~858K followers, 2,726+ posts.
- Mid-tier sourdough Instagram account. Borderline classification: Amy is named and occasionally on camera, but the post format is dominantly dough-and-hands rather than face-led.

## Format

Recipe Reels (overhead and hands-only), tutorial carousels with text-overlay step instructions, troubleshooting posts ("why your loaf is gummy"), occasional finished-loaf static photographs. The carousel pattern is particularly strong here — a single sourdough method walked through in 8–10 illustrated frames.

## Engagement signals

Saves-heavy account. Sourdough beginners use Amy Bakes Bread as a reference library, returning to the same posts to re-read instructions before a bake.

## Standout post patterns

- The "fix your loaf" carousel — common sourdough failure modes with photographs of each problem and the corrective action.
- The "schedule" carousel — a 24-hour sourdough timeline in card form, designed for screenshotting.
- The Reel of the dough during bulk fermentation, time-lapsed.

## What Homemade can take

- **Troubleshooting content has high saves rates.** A "why your loaf went wrong" carousel earns saves for years. Homemade's tutorial architecture already supports troubleshooter blocks (Phase 3a custom block); pulling those into Instagram carousel format is a direct port.
- **The 24-hour schedule visualisation.** A timeline that fits on a screen is screenshot-able and shareable. Equally relevant for the tomato sowing schedule, the cake fermentation schedule, the granny-square day plan.
- **Borderline-faceless can still work at scale.** Amy Bakes Bread proves that a named author can be largely absent from the camera without the audience defecting. The Homemade equivalent (a publisher with an editorial voice rather than a named author) is one step further in the same direction.

## What we'd do differently

- Caption discipline. Amy Bakes Bread captions sometimes carry the full recipe; Homemade keeps captions short and points to the tutorial page.
- Brand-direction styling consistency. The aesthetic ranges across posts; Homemade's locked palette and prop set should produce more visual unity.
