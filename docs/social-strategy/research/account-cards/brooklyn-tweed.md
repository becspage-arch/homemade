# Brooklyn Tweed

- **Handle:** @brooklyntweed
- **Founder:** Jared Flood (American yarn and pattern brand)
- **Primary platform:** Instagram
- **Cross-platform:** Own site at brooklyntweed.com (yarn + patterns), Ravelry, Pinterest
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Knitting / yarn brand, pattern publisher

## Scale and trajectory

- Instagram: ~175K followers.
- Pure brand-channel format — Jared founded the company but is not the recurring subject of posts. The yarn, swatches, and finished garments are.

## Format

Photographs of swatches, yarn skeins, finished garments. Editorial photography with strong styling: wood surfaces, woollen textures, neutral and earth-toned palettes. Captions are precise and informational: fibre composition, yarn weight, pattern context.

Posts are slower and more considered than typical Instagram pace — sometimes one post a week, more for pattern releases.

## Engagement signals

Audience is highly engaged and brand-loyal. Knitters who use Brooklyn Tweed yarn return repeatedly for new patterns and yarn releases.

## Standout post patterns

- The yarn close-up against wood or stone — texture-led photography.
- The garment-on-body in a natural setting — neutral environments, the knitwear styled into everyday life.
- The technical post — fibre information, gauge swatches, pattern construction notes.

## What Homemade can take

- **A yarn-brand voice is also a Homemade voice.** The technical precision combined with the photographic restraint is exactly the register Homemade should reach for in knitting and crochet.
- **Slower posting cadence is fine when the visual standard is high.** Brooklyn Tweed posts less than competitors but holds an engaged audience because every post is editorial-quality. Homemade can lean into quality-over-quantity in the textile niches.
- **The yarn-and-pattern + finished-garment + technique post mix.** A working content cycle: ingredient (yarn), recipe (pattern), result (garment), technique (deep dive). Same pattern works for cooking too.

## What we'd do differently

- The colour palette of Brooklyn Tweed leans towards heritage American (tweeds, navy, oatmeal) — Homemade's palette is locked elsewhere (sage, cream, terracotta, dusty blush, soft teal). Translate the visual discipline to the Homemade palette.
- Free pattern access. Brooklyn Tweed sells patterns; Homemade publishes them free.
