# PetiteKnit

- **Handle:** @petiteknit
- **Creator / brand:** Mette Wendelboe Okkels (Danish pattern designer)
- **Primary platform:** Instagram
- **Cross-platform:** Pattern shop, Pinterest, YouTube; own site at petiteknit.com
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Knitting / pattern design and finished garments

## Scale and trajectory

- Instagram: ~1.3M followers.
- The largest knitting Instagram account I identified in the brand-channel format. Borderline classification: Mette is named, but the format is dominantly garment-led, not designer-led.

## Format

Reels and photographs of finished knitted garments (jumpers, cardigans, hats, wraps) worn on bodies in styled environments. Close-up Reels of swatches, yarn, and finished stitches. Pattern releases produce content waves. The face of the designer is rare and the camera focuses on the garment.

Visual style is Scandinavian: muted palette, soft daylight, simple interiors, the knitwear styled as everyday wear rather than performance.

## Engagement signals

Pattern launches are the engagement events. Each new pattern produces a measurable spike. Saves are high — knitters bookmark the pattern Reel to return later.

## Standout post patterns

- The "model wearing the finished piece in a daylight kitchen" — a recurring visual archetype that defines the brand.
- The yarn close-up — swatches in natural light with the pattern name on screen.
- The pattern release Reel — quick montage of multiple finished versions worn by different people, in the same styled register.

## What Homemade can take

- **Garment-as-protagonist Instagram works at 1M+ scale in knitting.** The brand channel that shows the knitwear rather than the knitter is the proven model.
- **The Scandi visual register translates directly to Homemade's brand direction.** 70% English country plus 30% Scandi calm — PetiteKnit's photography is the Scandi reference.
- **Pattern releases as content moments.** Homemade can structure tutorial drops with the same launch-event cadence.
- **Cross-platform consistency.** PetiteKnit's Instagram, Pinterest, YouTube and own site all read as one brand. The Homemade brand direction enables the same coherence from launch.

## What we'd do differently

- "Free to use" positioning. PetiteKnit sells patterns. Homemade publishes them free; monetisation is downstream.
- Editorial-voice byline rather than designer byline. The Homemade knitting content is "by Homemade" rather than "by Mette."
- Slightly warmer styling. PetiteKnit reads cool-Scandinavian; Homemade pushes towards English-country-plus-Scandi warmth.
