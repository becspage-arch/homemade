# What to Cook

- **Handle:** carolinechambers.substack.com
- **Creator:** Caroline Chambers
- **Primary platform:** Substack
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Cooking / single-recipe weekly newsletter

## Scale and trajectory

- Substack: tens of thousands of subscribers.
- Ranked #1 in the The Clubb 2024 list of the most successful food Substacks. Personality-led — included here as a format reference, not a personality-replication target.

## Format

One complete-meal recipe delivered to inbox every Saturday morning. That's the entire product. Tagline: "One ridiculously impressive complete-meal recipe delivered to your inbox every Saturday morning."

The format constraint is the strategy. Subscribers pay (or read free) to outsource one cooking decision per week. The recipe is complete and the writing is concise.

## Engagement signals

The fact that the format constraint defines the brand, "one recipe, Saturday morning," is the headline insight. The audience knows what they're getting and treats the inbox arrival as a ritual.

## Standout patterns

- The fixed schedule. Saturday morning, every week, one recipe.
- The "complete meal" constraint. The whole meal: protein, sides, the lot.
- The writing is short. The recipe carries the content; the prose is supporting framing.

## What Homemade can take

- **A format constraint can be the brand.** Homemade's Substack could adopt a similarly disciplined structure — "one anchor tutorial, Saturday morning," "one seasonal recipe, Sunday evening." The constraint makes the inbox ritual sticky.
- **One specific decision outsourced per week.** Caroline's promise is "you don't have to think about dinner Saturday." Homemade can offer the equivalent for slow-living readers: "what to bake this weekend," "what to sow this week," "what to start this autumn."
- **Concise writing inside a strong format.** The voice doesn't have to be expansive when the recipe (or tutorial) is the substance.

## What we'd do differently

- Editorial-voice byline instead of personal byline.
- Multi-category rotation. Caroline is cooking-only; Homemade's weekly letter can rotate across the five categories with the consistency of the format holding it together.
