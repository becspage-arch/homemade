# King Arthur Baking Company

- **Handle:** @kingarthurbaking (Instagram), @kingarthurbakingcompany (TikTok)
- **Primary platform:** Multi-platform; website kingarthurbaking.com is the recipe and product home.
- **Cross-platform:** Instagram, TikTok (~144K), Pinterest, YouTube, Facebook, the King Arthur Baking School, an enormous email list.
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Baking / brand-channel publisher (heritage flour brand)

## Scale and trajectory

- Heritage flour brand founded 1790. Active social presence built around the free recipe library and the baker's hotline.
- Brand-channel format. Employees and named test-kitchen bakers feature occasionally but the brand identity is centred on the flour, the recipes, and the educational depth, not on a single personality.

## Format

Mixed format across platforms.

- **TikTok and Instagram Reels:** overhead recipe demos, technique shorts, ingredient explainers. Hands-only or hands-and-aprons; faces are occasional and never the focus.
- **Pinterest:** recipe pins driving to the recipe library.
- **YouTube:** longer-form technique videos, sometimes with a named test-kitchen baker but the visual identity stays brand-led.
- **Website:** the recipe-library-plus-product-shop model is the centre of the operation, with each social channel feeding back to it.

## Engagement signals

The Baker's Hotline (a free customer-service line staffed by experienced bakers) is a community-trust signal that translates into engagement on social. Audiences treat the brand as the authority and ask brand-channel comments with the expectation of expert reply.

## Standout patterns

- Free-recipe ethos. King Arthur Baking publishes recipes free at high volume without paywalling. This is the structural match for Homemade's "free to use" positioning.
- Educational depth. Recipes are accompanied by long-form technique articles (flour science, hydration, sourdough biology) that earn long-tail traffic.
- Branded measurements. Every recipe lists King Arthur Flour by name. Homemade's equivalent: linking recipes to glossary terms and sub-tutorials inside the same tutorial article.

## What Homemade can take

- **Heritage-brand voice in modern social formats.** King Arthur Baking sounds 200 years old and 5 years old simultaneously. The Homemade voice references (Alice Waters, Nigel Slater, Monty Don) plus the Tailwind / Fraunces / Lora visual stack can land the same way.
- **The educational-depth library + social distribution loop.** Each recipe earns long-tail Google traffic and seeds short-form social. Homemade's tutorial library plus the brand-channel social presence is the same loop.
- **Pinterest-first thinking for evergreen traffic.** King Arthur Baking has been pinning recipes since Pinterest existed. The cumulative pin authority is enormous.

## What we'd do differently

- King Arthur Baking sells flour and equipment. Homemade is publisher-first; monetisation comes via marketplace and premium tier later. The social strategy is therefore higher-funnel and less directly transactional.
- King Arthur Baking is American and skews American baking (cinnamon rolls, pancakes, cookies). Homemade is British, slow-living, English-country — adjust recipe selection accordingly.
