# Marf's Kitchen Garden

- **Handle:** @marfskitchengarden
- **Creator:** Martha (UK, London-based)
- **Primary platform:** TikTok and Instagram
- **Cross-platform:** Author of *Give it a Grow* (book); cross-platform presence
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Gardening / kitchen garden, urban, allotment-adjacent

## Scale and trajectory

- TikTok: ~478.5K followers.
- Instagram: ~215K followers.
- Borderline classification. Martha is the author and the name behind the brand, but the content reads as landscape-and-content-led rather than face-led — multiple sources describe the account as "landscape/content-led, eco-focused."

## Format

Overhead and three-quarter shots of London kitchen-garden beds and containers. Time-lapses of crops growing. Hands harvesting and planting. Generic narration on technique. Captions are concise and tutorial-led.

Audience is UK-rooted and skewed urban — the kitchen-garden-in-small-space niche.

## Engagement signals

Cross-platform audience (TikTok + Instagram) suggests a content strategy that pollinates between the two. Reels on Instagram and TikToks share source material.

## Standout patterns

- The London-kitchen-garden positioning. Most UK gardening content is rural or large-allotment; Marf's content speaks to readers without a country garden, which is a meaningful slice of the slow-living audience.
- The book-as-brand-anchor. Martha's name is on a book, which gives the social presence editorial authority.
- Eco angle (composting, no-dig, climate-aware sowing) runs throughout.

## What Homemade can take

- **UK-rooted faceless kitchen garden content works.** Marf's audience is the audience Homemade is building for.
- **The book-as-anchor model.** Homemade's equivalent is the tutorial library on homemade.education — every social post earns trust by linking back to deep tutorials.
- **Urban / small-space positioning as a sub-niche.** Homemade's gardening category can have explicit small-space content for renters and city readers.

## What we'd do differently

- Editorial-voice byline. Homemade's content is brand-channel; Martha's is personal. The format translates without the personal authorship.
- Brand-direction styling — sage-and-terracotta palette, Fraunces and Lora overlay typography. Marf's visual register is currently good but generic; Homemade can be more distinctive.
