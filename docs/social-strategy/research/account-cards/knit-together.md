# Knit Together

- **Handle:** littleskein.substack.com
- **Creator:** Anne Vally
- **Primary platform:** Substack
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Knitting / slow craft, weekly letter

## Scale and trajectory

- Substack: thousands of subscribers.
- Smaller scale than the food and gardening Substacks. The craft Substack space has a five-figure ceiling for most newsletters, but engagement runs high.

## Format

Weekly letter. The content register is "encouraging readers to slow down and knit or crochet more." Mix of pattern recommendations, technique notes, yarn-shop tours, trivia, product recommendations, and reflective writing on the rhythm of slow making.

The voice is warm, inclusive, unhurried. The tone references Erin Boyle's slow-living writing in adjacent newsletters.

## Engagement signals

Comments are warm and conversational rather than questioning — readers tell each other what they're knitting. The community is the retention.

## Standout patterns

- The "what I'm making this week" lede.
- The "yarn-shop tour" — virtual tours of independent yarn shops with descriptions and links.
- The "trivia and notes" — short, varied content that gives the letter shape without becoming a lecture.

## What Homemade can take

- **The slow-knitting voice is a brand-channel opportunity.** Anne's voice is the model; Homemade's editorial voice can occupy the same register.
- **Newsletter as community.** Even a smaller subscriber base produces meaningful engagement when the content invites response.
- **Cross-promotion of small independent yarn shops.** Homemade can do the same — features and tours of independent producers within the craft category. This is a discoverable angle that doesn't exist on the brand-channel side currently.

## What we'd do differently

- Editorial-voice byline. Homemade's knitting Substack content would carry "by Homemade" or a section editor's name, not a personal byline.
- Tighter sub-section structure. Anne's content blends multiple topics within a single letter; Homemade can be more clearly structured (each letter focuses on a single tutorial drop from the site, with secondary content as appendix).
