# Lalylaland

- **Handle:** @lalylaland
- **Primary platform:** Instagram
- **Cross-platform:** Pattern shop on Etsy and own site
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Crochet / amigurumi and decorative

## Scale and trajectory

- Instagram: ~160K followers.
- Brand-channel-style account in the decorative crochet space. The creator exists but content is dominantly object-led — finished pieces, process shots, pattern previews.

## Format

Static photographs of finished crochet pieces, dominantly. Carousel posts walking through a pattern step. Occasional Reels of process work. Photography is styled — neutral backdrops, natural light, the crochet piece as the subject.

## Engagement signals

High saves rate because each post can be a pattern reference. The Etsy / shop conversion loop appears strong — pattern releases drive measurable engagement spikes.

## Standout post patterns

- Object-led photography with the crochet piece centred and styled.
- Pattern release announcements with multiple-angle photographs.
- Seasonal collections — autumn pumpkins, Christmas baubles, Easter chicks.

## What Homemade can take

- **Brand-channel works in decorative crochet.** Object-led photography without a presenter can carry a 160K-follower Instagram in this niche.
- **Pattern releases as content events.** Each new pattern is a launch moment with multiple posts and reach. Homemade's tutorial publishing cadence can be marketed the same way — a new tutorial is a launch.
- **Seasonal collections structure the year.** Quarterly themed releases give the content calendar shape and the audience anticipation.

## What we'd do differently

- The Homemade aesthetic register is slightly different — English-country plus Scandi calm rather than bright decorative. Pattern selection should lean towards homeware (throws, cushions, table runners) and away from amigurumi for the launch period; amigurumi can re-enter once the broader brand voice is established.
- Free pattern access. Lalylaland sells patterns on Etsy. Homemade's "free to use" positioning means patterns are part of the free tutorial library.
