# GrowVeg

- **Handle:** GrowVeg (YouTube)
- **Voice:** Ben Vanheems (named, but the channel functions as a brand)
- **Primary platform:** YouTube — channel link via [The Garden Planner](https://www.growveg.com/)
- **Cross-platform:** GrowVeg.com website with planning tools and articles.
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Gardening / vegetable, tutorial-led

## Scale and trajectory

- YouTube: ~623K subscribers.
- Brand-channel format. Ben Vanheems narrates but the camera is dominantly on gardens, tools, and illustrated overlays — face-on-camera is rare and incidental.

## Format

Tutorial gardening videos with strong production values. Each video tackles a single technique or crop — "growing onions from sets," "intercropping in raised beds," "saving tomato seeds." Generic narration paired with garden footage, illustrated diagrams, and overlay text. The illustration overlays in particular are a structural pattern Homemade can directly replicate using its own illustration style.

Videos run 8–15 minutes typically. Pacing is calm and instructional rather than entertainment-led.

## Engagement signals

YouTube search intent: GrowVeg videos rank for technique queries (how to grow X, when to sow Y, what to do with Z in July). The search-driven audience is high-intent — they're already trying to do the thing.

## Standout video patterns

- The "month-by-month" series — what to do in the garden each month.
- The "crop guide" — a single vegetable from seed to harvest, in one 10-minute video.
- The "common mistakes" — five things people get wrong about a crop or technique.

## What Homemade can take

- **The illustrated-overlay tutorial format is the structural fit.** Homemade is already committed to illustration-first visuals. GrowVeg demonstrates that the same illustration approach can carry YouTube long-form gardening content.
- **YouTube search intent is the long-tail goldmine for gardening.** A "how to grow tomatoes" video published in March earns views for years on every gardener's search query in every March that follows.
- **Brand-channel-with-narrator is a viable model.** GrowVeg has a named narrator but his face is not the draw. Homemade can adopt the same pattern — an editorial voice that narrates without becoming the personality.

## What we'd do differently

- Visual identity stronger. GrowVeg illustrations are functional diagrams; Homemade illustrations are part of a locked brand-direction style. Tighter visual coherence than GrowVeg achieves.
- British growing year first. GrowVeg includes international content; Homemade's gardening content should be British-default for the launch period.
