# Joe's Garden

- **Handle:** @joesgarden
- **Primary platform:** TikTok
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Gardening / accessible fruit and veg growing

## Scale and trajectory

- TikTok: ~1.6M followers.
- One of the largest gardening accounts on TikTok globally; broadly accessible in register, sitting between American and British gardening styles.
- Borderline classification — Joe is named and the audience reaches partly through his recognisability, but the format leans hands-and-garden rather than face-led.

## Format

Mixed format. Garden walks, hands-on demonstrations, occasional pieces-to-camera. Tips-and-tricks tone for fruit and veg growing aimed at beginners. The garden itself is a strong recurring visual anchor.

The accessibility positioning is the brand: this is gardening for someone who's never grown anything before, framed without intimidation.

## Engagement signals

Mass reach. Comments are largely beginner-question-led, which suggests the audience is genuinely new to gardening rather than already expert.

## Standout patterns

- Single-tip videos. "Three things to do with your tomatoes this week."
- Garden tours showing the abundance of the harvest.
- Frequent reposting of evergreen content during relevant seasons.

## What Homemade can take

- **The mass-reach gardening TikTok audience exists and rewards accessibility.** Beginner-friendly content drives the bigger numbers. Homemade's beginner-mode toggle (already shipped) is a structural fit.
- **One garden as a recurring anchor.** The audience returns to see the same beds, same crops, same season cycle. Homemade can build that anchor via illustration even before a real-world garden is filmable.
- **Tips-and-tricks is fine as a format, even at depth standards.** The Homemade voice register can produce a "three things to do with your tomatoes this week" video that is concrete, specific, and brand-aligned.

## What we'd do differently

- Voice register. Joe's content is friendly-British-everyman; Homemade leans literary-warm. The pitch is one notch slower and one notch more considered.
- Slow living rather than accessibility-as-headline. Joe's positioning is "anyone can do this." Homemade's positioning is "this is worth taking seriously."
- Editorial byline rather than personal byline.
