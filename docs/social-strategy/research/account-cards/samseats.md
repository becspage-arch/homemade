# Sam Seats

- **Handle:** @samseats
- **Primary platform:** TikTok
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Cooking / ASMR-led, hands-only

## Scale and trajectory

- TikTok: large mid-to-upper-tier following (consistently listed in best-of TikTok food roundups).
- Recurring in aggregator lists of hands-only/overhead cooking accounts.

## Format

Hands-only, overhead and three-quarter angle. ASMR sound design carries the rhythm — chopping, sizzling, plating. Music either absent or quiet. Recipe focus tends toward visually rich dishes that produce strong process sounds (sandwiches, fried foods, anything with audible cuts or pours).

## Engagement signals

ASMR audience overlap. Comments tend to be sensory-led ("the sound when she cut into that…") rather than recipe-question-led.

## Standout patterns

- The sound-led intro. The first 1–3 seconds is the most satisfying audio moment in the recipe, used as the hook.
- Tight 30–60 second cuts. No long expositions.
- Plating shots that hold the camera fractionally longer than expected, giving the audience the savouring moment.

## What Homemade can take

- **ASMR-as-hook is platform-native and replicable.** The first 3 seconds carry the scroll-stop. Lead with the most audible moment in the cook.
- **Sound design is the cheapest production upgrade.** A clip-on microphone and a quiet kitchen produce 80% of the result. Far higher ROI than any visual production investment.
- **Plating-as-savouring.** Holding the camera on a finished dish for a moment longer than expected lifts the perceived quality without adding cost.

## What we'd do differently

- Recipe selection. The ASMR-favourite recipes are often fried, fast-food adjacent, or American-comfort-food. Homemade's slow-living British register pulls recipes from a different lane — sourdough scoring, jam jar pouring, preserved-lemon glints, hot pastry being cut.
- Brand-direction styling overlaid. Sam Seats's worktop styling is functional; Homemade's should be on-brand consistently.
