# Vittles

- **Handle:** vittles.substack.com
- **Editorial team:** UK / India online food magazine, multiple contributing writers
- **Primary platform:** Substack
- **Niche / sub-niche:** Food writing / brand-channel publisher

## Scale and trajectory

- Substack: subscriber count not publicly broken down, but listed among the most successful food publications on the platform.
- The single most relevant precedent in this entire research set: an explicitly brand-channel food publication on Substack, succeeding at scale in a category dominated by personal newsletters.

## Format

Long-form food writing and cultural criticism. Commissioned pieces from a rotating cast of writers — chefs, journalists, scholars, food workers. Topics span recipes, restaurant histories, food politics, regional cuisines, and the cultural infrastructure of eating. UK and India dual focus is distinctive.

Editorial voice is consistent across writers because the publication has clear style and standards.

## Engagement signals

Strong open rates and a paid tier that supports the editorial team. Cross-referenced in food media commentary as the brand-channel exception in a personality-led Substack ecosystem.

## Standout patterns

- The commissioned essay — a writer with relevant expertise on a single topic, thoroughly researched.
- The recurring series — features that return weekly or monthly with a fixed format.
- Cross-pollination between UK and India angles — neither one nor the other but a deliberate diaspora-aware editorial register.

## What Homemade can take

- **A brand-channel food publication can work on Substack.** Vittles is the rare proof. Homemade's positioning is different (broader homemaking; British-centred) but the structural argument (editorial voice over personal voice) holds.
- **Commissioned voice diversity within a consistent register.** When Homemade eventually adds creator contributions (Phase 6 onwards), the model is the same: multiple writers, one editorial standard.
- **Long-form earns paid tiers.** Vittles' paid subscribers pay for depth. Homemade's eventual premium tier follows the same logic.

## What we'd do differently

- Subject scope. Vittles is food-only; Homemade is cooking + baking + gardening + crochet + knitting. Broader scope, narrower per-piece depth at launch.
- Free tier emphasis. Vittles balances free and paid; Homemade's launch content is fully free, with monetisation deferred.
