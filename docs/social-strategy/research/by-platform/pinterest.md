# Pinterest

## Why Pinterest matters for Homemade

Pinterest is a search engine for visual ideas, not a social feed. The audience demographic (women, 30–60, planning meals and projects and gardens) is closer to Homemade's stated audience than any other platform. Pinterest pins are evergreen: a strong pin from year one can still drive traffic in year five. Personality is largely irrelevant. The pin and the destination are the brand.

Pinterest is also the safest platform for anonymous research. Pages are fully public, no login walls block the content, no bot-detection flags personal accounts.

## What's working for brand-channel accounts

### 1. Recipe / how-to publishers

**Food52** has ~288K Pinterest followers and over 10M monthly viewers across 100+ boards. Boards are organised by recipe type, occasion, and ingredient. Each pin is a single high-quality photograph plus a recipe-title overlay, linking back to the recipe page on food52.com.

**NYT Cooking** runs a brand-channel Pinterest account at scale. The brand presence is established but the parent NYT brand carries it; Homemade can't replicate the brand-equity transfer, but the format (one strong photo per pin, recipe title overlay, link to recipe page) is the universal pattern.

**BBC Good Food** — historically ~45K Pinterest followers (older data). Pinterest is BBC Good Food's biggest social referrer because the brand's photography style is "very visual." That's a relevant signal: Pinterest rewards visual taste over follower count, and a small follower base can still drive meaningful traffic when the pin design is right.

**King Arthur Baking Company** maintains a Pinterest presence alongside their TikTok (~144K) and Instagram (large). Brand-channel format, recipe-led, pins lead back to the recipe library on kingarthurbaking.com.

### 2. Long-tail pattern, technique and idea pins

Pinterest is where free crochet and knitting patterns live. The pattern aggregators (`allfreecrochet`, `nickishomemadecrafts`, the publishers Lion Brand and Hobbycraft) have built large Pinterest followings on a simple loop: pattern photo → link → free-pattern page → email signup.

For gardening, Pinterest pins on "what to plant in March," "companion planting chart," "seed-to-harvest timeline" are evergreen workhorses. A single chart pin can drive five-figure visits across multiple seasons.

### 3. Idea Pins (formerly Story Pins)

Pinterest's native multi-frame format. Recipe, project or growing-stage walk-throughs in 3–10 frames. Idea Pins don't link out and have weaker conversion economics than standard pins, but they push reach and the algorithm rewards posting them. Use sparingly — the standard pin is the conversion workhorse.

## Pin format that works

- Vertical aspect ratio, 1000 × 1500 pixels.
- Single dominant photograph (or illustration). Overhead or three-quarter angle. Natural light, warm tones, ceramic / linen / wood props. The brand-direction photography rules apply directly.
- One headline in a display serif. Tracked, sage on cream — matching the wordmark.
- The Homemade wordmark in a corner.
- One sub-line if needed (timing, season, or a hook detail).
- Pin description: 100–200 characters, recipe or technique name plus one specific detail. Keyword-rich but not stuffed.

## Boards strategy

The board is the canonical Pinterest unit. Pins live on boards, and boards rank in Pinterest search. A working Homemade board structure:

- One board per category (Cooking, Baking, Gardening, Crochet, Knitting).
- Inside each, sub-topic boards (Sourdough, Tomatoes, Granny Squares, etc.) once the pin volume justifies them.
- Seasonal boards (Spring Garden, Autumn Baking) populated each year.

Boards should be re-pinned over time, not abandoned. The Pinterest algorithm continues to surface pins from active boards.

## Hashtag and SEO patterns

Pinterest moved away from hashtags in 2020. The SEO surface today is:

- Pin title (60–100 characters).
- Pin description (with target keywords in the first 50 characters).
- Board title and board description.
- Image-recognition signals from the pin photograph itself.

Pinterest is the one platform where the brand can publish in heavy parallel (five to fifteen pins per day from a single source tutorial) without algorithm punishment, because pins are evergreen and surface over months not hours.

## Cross-posting in

The cheapest content stream is Pinterest from existing tutorial pages. Every tutorial published on homemade.education should produce three to seven pins automatically:

1. Hero illustration pin with the tutorial title.
2. A "what you'll need" pin if the supplies list is visual.
3. A "step X" pin from each major section.
4. A finished-result pin.
5. A glossary or chart pin if the tutorial includes one.

A bot in the tutorial publishing pipeline can generate these as soon as a tutorial is marked PUBLISHED.

## Verdict for Homemade

Strong fit. Pinterest is the platform with the highest leverage per unit of effort and the lowest creative bar (no video). Pin design is purely visual taste — and the brand direction already specifies the visual taste in extreme detail. Pinterest can be the first platform Homemade publishes on the day the splash gate drops.
