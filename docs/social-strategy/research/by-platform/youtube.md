# YouTube

## Why YouTube matters for Homemade

Long-form how-to is YouTube's native register, and it pairs well with Homemade's depth promise. Search intent on YouTube is high — people query for technique (how to make sourdough, how to plant tomatoes, magic-circle crochet) and reach the platform ready to learn. A faceless brand channel sits comfortably in the result set, often above personality-led channels for technical queries.

YouTube's secondary surface, Shorts, mirrors TikTok and can cross-pollinate from the same source video.

## The two formats that work without a face

### 1. The meditative overhead — Peaceful Cuisine / Cooking Tree model

Two channels define the genre.

**Peaceful Cuisine** (Ryoya Takashima, Japan) — 2.47M subscribers, started 2010. Vegan recipes, no face, no voice-over, no music. Just the sound of cooking, slow precise hands, beautiful styling. The format has been imitated worldwide and the parent channel still gains subscribers each month.

**Cooking Tree** (Korea): 5.2M subscribers. Desserts and café drinks. No face, no voice, no music. Pristine white kitchen, precise overhead shots, ASMR sound of eggs cracking and chocolate melting. Korean bakeries advertise their products as "Cooking tree style"; the channel created a visual language for an entire café category.

Both channels demonstrate that the absence of a personality is itself a positioning. The audience treats the videos as ambient — they're played in cafés and study rooms as background. The watch-time math is favourable: a 10-minute video that gets to 75% retention is sending the algorithm strong signals, and these videos do.

This is the closest analogue to where Homemade's video output should sit.

### 2. The instructional hands-only tutorial — Bella Coco / GrowVeg model

A second format, longer and more explicit, voice-narrated but with no face, works for skill-acquisition queries.

**Bella Coco** (Sarah-Jayne): 1.9M subscribers on YouTube. Crochet basics, technique tutorials, follow-along patterns. The video is shot on her hands, the voice-over narrates each step, the production is bright and clean. The face exists but is not the draw; the hands and the explanation are. A Homemade narrator with a similar voice-over discipline could produce content in the same lane.

**GrowVeg** (Ben Vanheems) — 623K subscribers. Tutorial gardening content with diagrams, hands-on shots, illustrated overlays. Brand-channel format ahead of personal-channel format. The author is named in the bio but the camera rarely shows him.

**Naztazia** (Donna Wolfe): 2.3M subscribers. Multi-craft (crochet, knitting, gardening, beading, sewing). Faceless and hands-led for the most part. Borderline (there is a recognisable voice) but the format is replicable with a different voice if needed.

**MilArt Marroquin** — 1.16M subscribers. Spanish-language crochet, follow-along visible. The format works across languages because the stitches are visible.

### What's notably out of scope

**So Yummy** (~10M subscribers) is technically hands-only but the content is hacks-and-clickbait. Wrong fit for Homemade's depth promise.

**Charles Dowding** (640–656K) is the gold-standard authority on no-dig gardening. He's on camera, but the format he's built (a slow tour of his garden, hands in the soil, technique-led voice-over) is structurally replicable without him. He's the format inspiration for an unbranded narrator standing in a (illustration-styled) garden montage.

**Joshua Weissman, Pro Home Cooks, Tasty** — all personality-led at scale. Skip.

## Hook patterns

YouTube rewards a different hook than TikTok. The thumbnail and title carry most of the click, and the first 15 seconds carry the watch.

- **Thumbnail:** the finished dish, garden bed, or finished crochet piece, photographed in the brand-direction style (overhead, soft warm light, ceramic and linen). No floating heads, no shocked-face reactions, no red arrows. Title in a serif overlay matches the wordmark.
- **Title:** specific, search-friendly, with one interesting detail. ("Tomato Sauce That Tastes Like Italy: Five Ingredients, 40 Minutes.") The duration claim is fine here — it's a factual machine duration ("40 minutes"), not a duration estimate for Rebecca's work.
- **First 15 seconds:** the finished result, then a single sentence of context (text overlay or generic narration), then straight into the process. No long brand intro. No "welcome back to the channel."

## Description and chapter patterns

Brand-channel YouTube descriptions are usually long. A working pattern:

1. Two-sentence summary of the technique or recipe.
2. Ingredients or supplies list (with metric measurements first, imperial in brackets).
3. Chapter timestamps.
4. Link to the full tutorial on homemade.education.
5. Source attribution if a public-domain recipe was adapted.

Chapter timestamps materially increase watch time and rank — viewers jump to the step they need and YouTube counts the engagement.

## Cadence

The Peaceful Cuisine / Cooking Tree cadence is roughly one long-form (8–15 minutes) per week, plus 2–5 Shorts cut from the source video. That's a sustainable cadence for a small team.

A weekly long-form anchored by a Saturday or Sunday upload pairs neatly with the slow-living audience pattern — that's when readers actually have time to bake.

## YouTube Shorts

Shorts are TikTok-equivalent and cross-pollinate cleanly. A 90-second cut from a 12-minute Peaceful-Cuisine-style video is the single most efficient piece of repurposing available on any platform. The same footage placed on TikTok, Reels and Shorts compounds the production effort threefold.

## Verdict for Homemade

Strong fit. The Peaceful Cuisine / Cooking Tree format is the single closest match to Homemade's brand voice across the entire research set — calm, deep, beautiful, no personality required, English-language opportunity wide open. This is the format to study and translate.
