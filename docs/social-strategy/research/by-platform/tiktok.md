# TikTok

## Why TikTok matters for Homemade

The strongest faceless creator pool in cooking and the most generous discovery for new accounts of any platform. Overhead-and-hands-only is now the default video grammar across baking, recipe demos, gardening time-lapse, and crochet stitch tutorials. The For You Page surfaces unknown accounts to relevant audiences without needing a follower base. A new brand-channel account can hit 100K followers on TikTok in months if the format is right.

## Hook patterns that recur

- First-frame: the finished thing, then immediately cut to the start. ("This is what we're making.")
- First-line on-screen text: the result with one specific surprising detail. ("Sourdough loaf that proves overnight in the fridge.")
- First-three-seconds sound: the most satisfying ASMR moment from the build (a knife on a tomato, a butter swipe, a dough slap). Then back to the start.
- No verbal hook. No "stop scrolling". The pattern that wins on Homemade-fit accounts is silence-plus-image, not speech.

## Caption patterns

Short. Two or three lines maximum. The first line is the recipe or technique name. The second is an interesting fact or substitution note. The third, if present, is a quiet CTA: "full recipe via my bio" or "saving this for later." Long captions sit below the fold and rarely earn the scroll.

## Hashtag patterns

TikTok hashtags still meaningfully influence discovery, unlike Instagram. A working set is:

- 1 broad: `#cooking` `#baking` `#gardening` `#crochet` `#knitting`
- 1 mid-niche: `#sourdoughbaking` `#kitchengarden` `#crochettutorial`
- 1 tight: `#scoredough` `#allotmentlife` `#granny-square` etc.
- 1 brand: `#homemadeeducation`

Three to five tags total, not the wall of fifteen that worked in 2020.

## Cadence

Successful brand-channel accounts in cooking TikTok post three to five times per week, with bursts of seven to ten during a topical moment (Christmas baking, sourdough revival, tomato season). Below two posts per week the algorithm stops surfacing new content reliably.

## Notes on TikTok-specific risks

- Music licensing: commercial accounts cannot use the full music library that personal accounts can. The Commercial Music Library is smaller and skews instrumental, which suits the calm-overhead format anyway.
- Sound-first viewing: a majority of TikTok views are sound-on. A silent-aesthetic post can still work but it must have either text or ambient sound carrying the rhythm.
- TikTok Shop is now visible in feed in the US and UK. Brand-channel accounts can leave it disabled at this stage â€” it doesn't suit Homemade's positioning.

## Verdict for Homemade

Strong fit for cooking and baking. Plausible fit for gardening with the overhead time-lapse format. Weaker fit for knitting and crochet on TikTok specifically â€” those crafts work better on YouTube and Pinterest for non-personality-led formats.
