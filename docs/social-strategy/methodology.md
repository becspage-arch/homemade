# Methodology

How this dossier was built. Read this first if you want to know how seriously to take any specific number or claim.

## Filter applied to every account

An account is in scope only if its content could be reproduced by Homemade today, with no specific human on camera and no distinctive personal voice carrying it. Concretely:

- Overhead / top-down video, hands-only video, stop-motion, time-lapse, static photography with overlay text, text-led video with no voice-over, generic narration, diagram / educational visual, aggregator re-shares, brand-channel accounts where a person exists behind the camera but the format does not depend on that person.
- Out of scope: anything where the face, voice, or personality of a named creator is the draw. Even when the content is excellent.

Borderline cases are flagged in the account card. The bar is replicability, not aesthetic.

## Sources

Three layers, in priority order:

1. **Marketing-blog aggregators and roundups.** Feedspot, Modash, IZEA, Tasting Table, Russh, Puratos, Influencer Hero, The Social Shepherd, Tatler, Eat Your Books, Craft Industry Alliance, Spoon University, etc. Used to surface candidate accounts and approximate follower counts. These vary in freshness — most figures are from late 2025 or 2026, but a few are older.
2. **Trade and publisher commentary.** Slate, Big Issue, The Drum, FIPP, Statista, taste-cooking.com, the Lever, KoreaChannels. Used to validate that a creator or pattern is genuinely working, not just listed.
3. **Public platform pages.** Substack home-page rankings, Pinterest profile pages (anonymous), TikTok and YouTube channel pages (anonymous). Used to confirm format and a recent post sample where the third-party data was thin.

Social Blade was queried for growth trajectory on the headline accounts. The site returned 403 to automated requests during this session, so the figures cited in the cards come from the marketing-blog and trade-publication sources above. The Social Blade URLs are included in each card so Rebecca can pull a live trajectory chart in a browser when she wants to verify.

## What was deliberately not done

- No logged-in social platform navigation. Rebecca's personal accounts were not used and the browser MCPs were not invoked. The constraint is from the worker brief: bot detection on Instagram and TikTok can flag a personal account even when behaviour is benign.
- No purchasing of analytics tools (HypeAuditor, Modash full reports, Tubular) — only their free aggregator pages were read.
- No outreach to creators.

## What to treat as solid vs. tentative

**Solid:**

- Format claims (a given account is overhead-and-hands-only, or face-on-camera). These were sourced from multiple aggregators describing the account, plus the account's own bio text on public pages where available.
- Order-of-magnitude follower counts (100K vs. 1M vs. 10M). Cross-referenced across at least two aggregator sources for every headline account.
- The cross-niche pattern claims in `synthesis.md`. These appear in multiple independent sources.

**Tentative:**

- Exact follower counts. Many sources are months old. Verify on Social Blade or the platform before citing externally.
- Growth-rate claims. Limited Social Blade access in this session means month-over-month growth is asserted only where multiple sources agreed it was rising.
- Engagement rates. Not collected systematically — marketing aggregators rarely publish like-to-follower ratios.

**Speculative:**

- Anything in the playbook about how a given content template will perform for Homemade specifically. That's a hypothesis to test against real posting data, not a prediction.

## Niche coverage notes

- **Cooking and baking:** richest faceless creator pool. Plenty of large, well-documented accounts on TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest, Instagram. Substack space is largely personality-led with one brand-channel exception (Vittles).
- **Gardening:** strong on TikTok (overhead garden tours, time-lapse), YouTube (technique-led walks), Pinterest (seed-to-harvest pin sequences). Instagram in the UK skews heavily personality-led, particularly around the Chelsea Flower Show / RHS partnership content.
- **Crochet:** TikTok is dominated by personality-led creators with finished-object reveals. The faceless format that works at scale is YouTube hands-only stitch tutorials. Pinterest is enormous for free patterns.
- **Knitting:** the sparsest niche for non-personality-led content. Major knitting creators run podcasts or YouTube vlogs where the face and voice are central. The faceless alternatives are pattern-brand accounts (PetiteKnit, Brooklyn Tweed) which show garments and process rather than the designer.
- **Cross-niche:** Substack overall is a personality-driven platform; brand newsletters underperform individual writers. This shapes the Substack play in the playbook.

## Voice discipline

All output in this folder follows the brand-voice rules in `feedback_homemade_voice.md` and Section 6b of the brand direction doc. Banned phrases were checked and removed before each file landed. The em-dash rule (max one per paragraph) is observed in all narrative prose. Tables and bullet lists may use longer constructions where clarity demands it.
