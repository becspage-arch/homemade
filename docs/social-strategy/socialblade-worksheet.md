# Social Blade verification worksheet

The research in this folder relies on follower counts pulled from marketing-blog aggregators. Those numbers are vanity metrics — they tell us where an account is, not whether it's still growing. Rebecca's exactly right that this matters: a creator who peaked in 2022 and has been flat since is the wrong template for an account starting from scratch in 2026.

This worksheet is the source-of-truth list. Each account below needs a live Social Blade check before we act on the playbook.

## How to do the check

For each account, open the Social Blade link below in your browser. The page is fully public, no login needed. Record four numbers:

1. **Δ 30 days** — change in subscribers / followers over the last 30 days. Positive = growing, negative = declining, near zero = stalled.
2. **30-day views** — total video / pin views in the last 30 days. Confirms the channel is still actively watched, not just retaining a back-catalogue audience.
3. **Last post / video date** — the most recent upload. Anything more than 60 days old means the account is going dormant.
4. **Grade** — Social Blade's letter grade in the top-right of the page (A+ through D-). Their proprietary number; useful as a sanity check.

Then write a one-word verdict: **growing** / **flat** / **declining** / **dormant**.

## What to do with the filled-in worksheet

Once Rebecca has populated even half of this:

1. Sort the rows by the **Δ 30 days** column. The accounts at the top of the list are currently growing; the accounts at the bottom are dormant or declining.
2. **The growing accounts are the templates we should actually study.** A dormant 2M-follower account isn't a useful model for our launch; a 200K-follower account that gained 30K subscribers in the last month is.
3. The **declining or dormant** accounts can stay in the research as historical context but should be deprioritised in the playbook. The playbook's first-90-days calendar should weight toward formats and platforms where today's working accounts are still gaining audience.

Once we have the data, we revisit `playbook.md` together and rewrite the lead-platform recommendation and the first-90-days calendar with the growth signals as the deciding evidence.

## A note on what Social Blade can't tell us

Social Blade reports follower trajectory. It doesn't report:

- **Audience composition.** A 30K-subscriber growth wave could be one viral video that brought in low-intent followers who don't engage. Pair the Δ numbers with a quick scroll of the channel to see whether content quality is consistent.
- **Engagement rate.** Likes-per-follower and comments-per-follower can fall as a channel grows. Worth a glance in the recent posts.
- **Demographics.** Whether the audience is the slow-living-30–55 demographic Homemade serves, or e.g. teenagers, is invisible from outside. Best inferred from the comment sections.
- **Sustainability.** A 30-day burst doesn't tell us whether the growth is sustainable. The 6-month and 1-year Social Blade charts on each page are more informative than the headline 30-day number.

When in doubt, the 6-month chart on the Social Blade page is the single most useful view. Look for a steady upward slope, not a single spike.
