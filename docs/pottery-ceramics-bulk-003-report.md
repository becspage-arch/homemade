## Anti-tells / common-issues additions

Three patterns recurred across the 23 fixed files. Worth flagging back to `docs/pottery-ceramics-author.md` or `docs/common-issues.md`:

1. **Troubleshooter `fix` strings drift above grade 12.0.** Authors tend to compress troubleshooting answers into long single sentences with technical vocab. The grade-level rule fires on ~half the troubleshooter blocks. Suggested fix: anti-tell entry — "Troubleshooter `fix` strings must be ≤ 2 short sentences each, grade ≤ 12.0. Don't chain causes inside the fix; that's the `cause` field's job."

2. **"Before you start" as a section heading triggers safety-block.** Authors reach for this heading naturally when prepping the reader. Suggested fix: prefer "Preparation" or "Setting up" as the standard heading; fold any safety lines into a numbered step.

3. **"Anchor" and "Target" as common false positives.** Both are common pottery verbs (anchor a coil; target wall thickness). Worth a one-line note in common-issues so future authors know they're warnings, not errors, and don't waste self-critique cycles trying to rewrite around them.

These aren't added to common-issues.md or anti-tells in this run — flagging for the next pipeline-maintenance pass.
