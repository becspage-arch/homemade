# Test subjects

Six subjects chosen to stretch different visual capabilities. Each one is
something cooking illustrations actually need to do. A winning style holds
together across the full set.

The subject descriptions below are the prose you append to whichever style
prompt prefix (A, B, C, D) you're testing. The full prompt is always:

```
<style prompt prefix from styles.md>, <subject description below>
```

---

## 01 — A finished food item

**Slug:** `01-sourdough-loaf`

**Description:**

A round country-style sourdough loaf, golden-brown crust scored with a wheat
pattern across the top, sitting on a wire cooling rack. A linen tea towel
underneath. Soft window light from the left.

**Visual challenge:** texture. The crust has to read as crackled, the
crumb is implied through the scoring. A flat-looking loaf undermines the
whole tutorial.

---

## 02 — A technique mid-action

**Slug:** `02-folding-dough`

**Description:**

A pair of hands folding bread dough on a wooden board, dough mid-stretch,
flour dusted across the surface, sleeves rolled to the forearms. Side-on
view, the action caught mid-motion.

**Visual challenge:** action. AI illustration often fails at hands and at
implied motion. If the style can't render hands, the whole technique
library is undermined.

---

## 03 — A still life of supplies

**Slug:** `03-preserving-supplies`

**Description:**

A still life of preserving equipment laid out on a kitchen table: a Kilner
jar, a brass kitchen scale with two weights, a glass funnel, a square of
muslin / cheesecloth folded loosely, a wooden spoon, a small saucer.
Overhead three-quarter angle.

**Visual challenge:** multiple objects in one frame holding consistent
weight and not looking cluttered. The supplies-card illustrations and the
hero illustrations for many preserves tutorials look like this.

---

## 04 — A botanical / ingredient illustration

**Slug:** `04-rosemary-sprig`

**Description:**

A single sprig of rosemary, leaves and stem detailed, against a plain warm
cream background. Slight shadow underneath. The kind of botanical drawing
that would sit happily in a vintage gardening book.

**Visual challenge:** linework precision. Botanical illustrations need a
specific kind of restraint. This subject also tests how the style handles a
single object against negative space — important for inline illustrations
sitting next to body text.

---

## 05 — A diagram

**Slug:** `05-fermentation-diagram`

**Description:**

A labelled cross-section diagram of a jar of fermenting sauerkraut at three
different stages of fermentation, shown as three jars side by side. Each
jar shows the cabbage, the brine line, and the bubble activity. Labels in a
serif typeface point to the brine line, the bubble layer, and the weighted
plate. Style: technical but warm, like an old herbal or domestic manual.

**Visual challenge:** labelling and information density. Diagrams have to
do a job that a hero image doesn't. The style has to hold the cross-section
clarity without going full corporate-vector.

---

## 06 — A scene

**Slug:** `06-morning-kitchen`

**Description:**

A kitchen counter at morning light. A wooden chopping board with a folded
linen cloth on it. A copper pan to one side. A sheet of parchment paper
with a dusting of flour. A clear glass of water. The window in the
background is implied rather than rendered — the light is what's important.

**Visual challenge:** mood. This is the kind of illustration that opens a
whole sub-category index page. It has to feel like a place a real person
cooks in. AI defaults to either over-styled food photography or empty
geometric scene, and neither will read as Homemade.
